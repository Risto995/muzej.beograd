BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 27)
PLAN OF ORSAVA (ORSOWA)

Plan of Orsava with close environs. The fortification consists of three parts: ramparts on the left bank of Danube reinforced by water-ditch, ramparts on the hillside above the right Danube bank, and a fortification on island (allegedly called Caroline). Orsava is surrounded by following representations: B) abandoned Turkish station, C) position of Austrian cavalry, and D) infantry; west of the place is an elevation E). A description, at both sides of the plan, notes that this place is located on the junction of three borders, Hungarian, Transylvanian and Wallachian. In 1688 , immediately after the occupation of Belgrade, the local Serbs and Wallachians (Romanians) revolted against the Turks, then follows a description of subsequent events which ended in 1716, when Orsava was taken over by Austrians, after Pančevo and Stara Banatska palanka . In the bottom right is an explanation with A - I entries, and number 134 is marked at the top right corner.
