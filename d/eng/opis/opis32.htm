BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 12)
PLAN OF PETROVARADIN (PETERWARDEIN)

The plan of Petrovaradin is oriented N-S, according to the mark in the upper left corner. Representations are as follows: fortified town, castle with courtyard bastion, along with inner and outer bastion lines heading westward, both reinforced by "Spanish rhombuses". South of the town, outside the ramparts, there is a fortress and settlement of Maierhoff. Town walls and fort ramparts which face the Danube were not yet erected. Two bridges connect it to the right bank of Danube and the roads to Futog and Szeged.

Just on the right bank of Danube , across the castle, there is Hrvatsko selo (Croatian village) and a little farther, beyond coastal marshlands, Srpska varoš (Serbian town, Ratzenstadt, future Novi Sad). A short description and history of Petrovaradin is placed on the left side of the picture. It is noted that the town was conquered in 1687, and that the fortress was subsequently renovated and reinforced thanks to general Caprianni. Finally, the Turks have besieged the town in August 1716, but were defeated in a big battle by the army commanded by Eugene of Savoy .

Above the upper right corner is the number 143.
