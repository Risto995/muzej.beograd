BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 8)
PANORAMA OD MONGATS (MUNKATSCH)

Panorama of the town surrounded by water. The settlement is located on a plateau below the rock, irregularly organized and protected by palisades with several towers built in masonry. A wooden bridge leads to the town, defended by two towers. As regards the settlement, a church with pointed belfry stands to the right of the bridge and to its left is a church with a dome above the belfry - probably an orthodox one. A four-level fort is situated on the rock above the town. Its first two levels contain the baracks. A short narrative in the upper left corner of the picture explains that it was an old town and that Hungarian kings and Transylvanian dukes argued about it. The town with modern Hungarian name of Munkacs is actually Mukačevo placed eastward of Čop, now in Ukraine.

Above the right corner of the picture is the number 108.
