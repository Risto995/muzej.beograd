LAUDON'S CONQUEST OF BELGRADE

A view of Belgrade seized on 30 September 1789 by Fieldmarshall Laudon from the land. The picture represents the siege of Belgrade, with arrangement of objects and viewpoint almost identical to the work of Shitz-Mansfeld (I1/468). Austrian senior officers are in the foreground, and in the bottom stands a cavalryman (Fieldmarshall Laudon ?) in front of the large scroll showing the plan of Belgrade. The lined up troops are placed further toward the town, followed by approaches and armed force in action. A large mosque is in the center as well as a windmill to its right. The legends are lacking.
