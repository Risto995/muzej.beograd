THE SIEGE AND CONQUEST OF BELGRADE IN OCTOBER 1789

Representation of the siege of Belgrade seen from the southern, mainland side, in the moment when Austrian army advances through the already occupied suburb and assails the Upper town (5). Battle camp of the besiege forces is represented in the foreground with lined up troops and the artillery positions arranged. Commander of the siege Fieldmarshall Laudon (2) is also depicted, and Belgrade burning under artillery fire is in the background. A short history of the siege is placed below the picture, along with a legend with 10 entries (1 -10) in French (to the left) and German (to the right). Number 46 is marked above the upper right corner, implying that the sheet probably came from an album about the wars of Austrian Emperor Francis , who was a member of Fieldmarshall Laudon 's headquarters at the time when he was a young prince.
