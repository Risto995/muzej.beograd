VIEW OF BELGRADE AT THE TIME OF TURKISH ATTACK IN 1521 (AFTER RESCH)

We suppose that the sheet was torn off from an edition of Muenster's Cosmography (Cosmographia universalis). The prototype of this panorama is a woodcut by Resch with representation of Belgrade attacked by Turkish forces - Suleiman the Magnificent. Even the error was copied, because the drawing was not cut inversely (as if reflected in mirror). The panorama is on page 1368. At the top stands the following text: The sixth book (Das Sechste Buch) about the siege of Belgrade (griechisch Weissenburg belagert). Watermill on the bank of Sava (Saw), Turkish yawls on Danube (Tonaw fl.), towers and turrets in the Upper town and a large church in the center of Lower town are all clearly visible. Below the picture is a text (22 rows) about the siege of Belgrade beginning with a fact that anno Domini 1457 (an error - 1456), the Turks besieged Belgrade. A description of battles which took place before the conquest of town in 1521 then follows. A history of the Christian-Turkish conflicts in South-East Europe since 1396 is represented on page 1367, as well as a response of the king Sigismund (Žigmund, Sigosmund).
