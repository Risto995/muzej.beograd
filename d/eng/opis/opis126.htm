DRAFT OF THE IMPERIAL CAMP BELOW BELGRADE

A representation of the Imperial camp below Belgrade on 23 July, when (Belgrade?) was bombarded for the first time from the river. The besieged Belgrade is depicted quite schematically. The Upper town is marked (as Belgrade), the Lower town (Water town, Vodena varoš - Wasser Statt * with error) - in fact the northwestern zwinger, the Danube port (Haven) and Serbian suburbs (Reitzen Vorstatt * with error). A graphic mark for Zemun (Zemlin) is placed in the upper left, with a note that the Turks burned it during the retreat. The position of count Hauben's corps on the left bank of Sava is represented with regiments under the command of: Mersi, Lofelhol, Ansbah, Lotring, Karafa, Hessen, the Bavarians, the Darmstadtians, and the regiments of Eugenee of Savoy , Lobković and Martini. The position of troops consisting of 10 grenadier companies and 1.100 cavalrymen is shown on the left bank of Danube . Having crossed the bridge on Dunavac under the command of count Mersi on 11 July, they attacked Turkish redoubt at the mouth of Dunavac. Location of the village of Višnjica (Wisniza) is marked on the right side. The largest part of picture is occupied by representations of the besiege camp with bridgeheads, circumvallation and contravallation lines, the main headquarters, artillery and everything that is required for a besiege. The following regiments are marked: Harah - defending the Sava bridgehead, Holstein, Scheborn, Hotois, Gontrecour, Kardi and Colern on the left wing; Frid. Wurtemberg, Marulli, Alaudet, Upper Lorraine, Trautzon, and young Daton and Bevern in the center and on the right wing toward Belgrade. Reserve regiments were commanded by: Emanuel of Savoy - the chief commader's brother, Vasquez, Graven, Villard, Gelbes and Bati. Turkish attack from the south had to be received and responded by the following regiments: Heister, Palfy, Herberstein, Savoy , Felen, Jerger, Gronfeld, Falkenstein, Croix, Durlach, Banyi, Maximilian Staremberg, Fezel, Sikingen, Boneval, Braun, Ahumada, Regal, Virmont, Old Wurtemberg, Old Lorraine, Lobković, Martigny, Darmstadt, Hanover, Radutin, Altheim, Wurtemberg, Geschwind, and Guido Staremberg. Two pontoon bridges (Schiffbruck) are constructed over Sava. Downstream of the Lower town, a group of 8 Turkish yawls floats on Danube , with or without sails. It is noted that they attacked two Imperial boats near Zemun on 6 July. Three large ships (corvette?) St. Eugeneee, St. Leopold and St. Elisabeth are marked at Zemun . Upstream of the pontoon bridge on Danube are the battle corvettes St. Joseph and St. Francis, and downstream of it are the three unnamed battle ships. A unit commanded by Otto Staremberg defends the access to the bridge on the left bank of Danube .
