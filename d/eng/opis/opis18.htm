ALBUM OF THE TREATY OF POŽAREVAC (FIG. 16)

In contrast to previous scenes which represented integral units, this sheet contains two separate scenes:

A) left: An opened tent with a group of people inside and the quard in front of it. The tent belongs to Imperial (Austrian delegation) and there is an "apple" at its top. In the left rear of the tent are the Turks. Looking downwards there are: Ibrahim-pasha, beylerbey of Rumelia , a) his interpreter, c) pashas and aghas from his escort, and e) janissaries . Imperial delegation is on the right. Right at the top is count Oduyer, and next to him are b) his interpreter, d) gentlemen patricians and officers and f) grenadirs from the company of the prince Alexander of W�rttemberg. Right in the entrance of the tent g) is the horse of Turkish negotiator with two stableboys surrounded by grenadirs .

B) right: An opened Turkish tent with a crescent at the top, and a group of people in its interior. Imperial representatives are on the left, and the Turks are on the right. Emissaries - negotiators are placed at the raised platform approachable by a carpet. The following persons are represented there: 1) Imperial interpreter, 2) gentlemen patricians and officers, and 7) general Oduyer's horse held by two seyzes (stableboys). On the right side are: 3) Turkish interpreter, 4) pashas and aghas, 5) janissaries , and 6) three Turkish carpets covering the floor and a postament with seats for negotiators.

At the top of both pictures there are extensive explanations in German language. For the scene represented on the left it is stated that it shortly describes paying the first visit of Turkish emissary to count Oduyer on 27 June 1719, which took place in a tent erected for that occasion. After the reception, Turkish emissary received gifts worth 3.500 gulden. In return he offered a gift of 12 rolls of brocade. In the legend for the picture on the right, a subsequent meeting of emissaries is noted, which took place one day later, on 28 June, in the Turkish camp. The tent and equipment are described as well as the gift offered to the count on that occasion: a horse with luxurious equipment and a saber.
