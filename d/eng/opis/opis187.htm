PORTFOLIO OF PICTURES OF THE GREAT \OR\E PETROVI] KARA\OR\E (FIG.2)
PORTRAIT OF KARA\OR\E

A portrait of the whole figure of Kara|or|e in folk costume, with raised saber, two pistols ( kubura ) stuck in silah , and a rifle. Cut off heads of the Turks are below his feet along with battle trophies and enchained Crescent. Vertical frieze at his right represents the enchained girl (Serbia) and an eagle releasing her from chains. Below the girl is a sphere with laurel wreath encircled by the following text: Kosovo on 15 (28) June 1389. Below all the noted depictions stands a church (St. Sophia ?) turned into the mosque. To the left of Kara|or|e stands another, identical vertical frieze with armed girl wearing laurel wreath on her head, holding a banner and armour of Serbia (double headed crowned eagle and upon its chest an armour with cross and four strikers (metal object for striking flint in order to obtain fire sparks). Below this girl stands a sphere embroidered by the wreath of oak leaves inscribed "Topola 24 January (6 February). A Christian temple is depicted below the text, in the style of Serbian Baroque. Both friezes are interconnected by the structure with Royal crown at its top and a symbol of the Holy Trinity below it.
