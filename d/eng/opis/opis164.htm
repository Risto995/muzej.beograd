PANORAMA OF BELGRADE FROM THE PERIOD OF AUSTRIAN GOVERNMENT IN 1717-1739

Representation of a broad panorama of Belgrade (identical to the one preserved in album I1/5039, even the fold is marked), viewed across the confluence of Sava and Danube , so that Veliko ratno ostrvo (Great War island) remains in the foreground. The inner fortification of the Upper town is marked besides a castle (1), northeastern and northwestern zwinger (5 and 4), commander's residence in the center of Lower town (6), and the main mosque (7) to the far right. An explanation in German language with entries (1 - 9) is in both corners below the engraving, and right at the bottom is the signature of G. Bodenehr, the publisher. Between two parts of the legend is a text about Belgrade, its name (antique name for the town was erroneously changed for antique name of Zemun " Taurunum "), and its history until the glorious victory of Eugenee of Savoy in 1717 .

The engraving was probably a part of large publication of Gabriel Bodenehr, an album with more than 200 panoramas of the most important European towns, published in several volumes under the joint title "Europas Pracht und Macht". The text in German language says: Belgrade or Greek White town.
