A VIEW OF BELGRADE IN THE MID XIX CENTURY

A view of Belgrade from the left bank of Sava in the vicinity of its mouth, so that the fortress and a part of settlement until harbour, on the terrace of Sava are seen. Austrian frontier guard is in the foreground, passed by a carriage with passengers moving down to the river bank. The engraving was probably made immediately after 1862, regarding a presence of the large esplanade around fortress, which was created in the above noted year according to the agreement of Kanlidža, in order to prevent conflicts between Serbs and Turks by separation of Turkish fortress from Serbian settlement. The title: Turkish fortress Belgrade (in German language) - Belgrade (in Serbian Cyrillic letters prior to Vuk's reform) is placed below the picture).
