BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 1)
SYMBOLIC REPRESENTATION OF HUNGARY IN THE SHAPE OF ROMAN EAGLE, AND EXPULSION OF THE TURKS

Two thirds of the sheet are occupied by the scene representing a battle eagle resting on ruins. A title in the upper central part says:"The kingdom of Hungary released from Turks by the Roman eagle - i.e., the latest plans and views of all important towns, forts, rivers and roads in the Kingdom of Hungary and the neighbouring countries, along with the history of each place from the earliest times until today. The remaining (subsequently coloured) right part of the sheet, represents the scene of Turks removing only the most necessary equipment in panicked flight from a building on fire, i.e., Europe.
