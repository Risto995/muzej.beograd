THE INSPECTION OF AUSTRIAN ARMY NEAR ZEMUN

The inspection was organized in May, 1788. In the central part of the engraving are the following representations: the Emperor Joseph II (1), Archduke Francis (2), fieldmarshall Lasi (3) and a group of senior officers (4, 5, 6). Behind them, under unfolded flags, wearing high fur hats ("čak"), are the members of Hungarian courtly guard, and to the far right is the detachment of heavy cavalry - the cuirassiers (9). To the left, between the lined up grenadirs (14) and the Imperial headquarters is the group of (Serbian) volunteers (11) is passing by on horses. Belgrade (17) is represented in the rear, to the left, and the renewed Zemun (19) is depicted at its right. As concerns the settlements, the following sites are noted: Crvenka (Zervinka - 25), the village of Banovci (Banofze - 26), and Kraljevo (Kraljova - 27), probably the village of Kraljevci in the vicinity of Ruma. The picture is provided with legend with 27 entries in French language (in its upper part) and in German (in the lower part).
