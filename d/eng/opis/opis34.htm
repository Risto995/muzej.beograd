BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 14)
PLAN OF THE SIEGE OF TIMISOARA (TEMESWAR)

The plan of Timisoara shows a small town ( palanka ) placed on the left bank of Timis, fortified by palisades , with a fortified castle behind it which rests upon the riverbank. On the left bank are: A) town, B) small town ( palanka ), N) Srpska varoš. The camp of Christian army which besieged the town is placed to the west and east of the town; in this way the army interrupted the roads to Arad and Csanad. North of the town, a large semi-circle notes the line of siege, i.e., contravallation line with excavated trenches leading to the town E), artillery positions H), and a large fortification - the redoubt K). At each side of the plan there are town descriptions and history. Final historical notes are dedicated to the siege and conquest of Timisoara on 1-12 December 1716. At the right bottom is a short explanation with entries A-N.
