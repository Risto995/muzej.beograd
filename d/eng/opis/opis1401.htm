Representation of the gloomy winter landscape, with hills covered by snow in far background. In the foreground to the right stands semi- interred object of stone and earth, covered by earth. Its entrance is to the far right. Human skull is placed upon the cover, with (curiously) preserved collar of the shirt and upper part of dressing. It could belong to a priest, skin taken from his head and, according to ancient Turkish custom, filled with hay and then sent to the command in charge. Remnants of the victim remained on the noted shed.

Note on the back. "Turkish shed near Kosnik"
