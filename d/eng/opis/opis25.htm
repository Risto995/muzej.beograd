BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG.5)
ISOMETRIC PLAN OF SZIGET

The plan shows an accurate position of Sziget in the marshlands at the north of Drava. The town is placed on three islands. At the largest, middle one, the old town is placed; on the smaller island on its left is the new part of town, and on a thoroughly fortified island on the right is the fortress with a castle, but also a mosque is perceptible. The islands are interconnected by bridges, and a bridge linking the old town and fortress is additionally reinforced by fortified gate. Historical facts are noted on the left side of the picture, and also on the right side in few lines. They begin with an explanation about the town location. However, the largest part of text deals with Turkish siege layed by Suleiman I the Magnificent in 1566, and the heroic resistance of Nikola Zrinjski (Serini). It ends with a note that the town fell under Christian command in 1689, after two years of blockade. The explication contains entries from A to D. Number 153 is marked at the top, on the right.
