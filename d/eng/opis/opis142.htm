BATTLE SCENE OF THE IMPERIAL CONQUEST OF BELGRADE ON 6 SEPTEMBER 1688

The largest part of the engraving is dedicated to the final assault and conquest of Belgrade on 6 September 1688 , and its defense by Turkish crew. The assault of Christian cavalry and a group of captured janissaries pulling the cannon under supervision of Maximilian Emmanuel are represented in the lower left corner. A group of Christians, the inhabitants of Belgrade, represented on the right side, are watching the victorious advance of besieging forces from the walls, in prayer and delight. The brutality of this conflict is testified by Turkish heads which were cut off and stuck on cuirassier spears, and fixed upon the already taken ramparts. The appearance of Belgrade in this engraving is not realistic, as it reminds to some north German, Flemish, or Dutch town. Even the towers in Belgrade are represented with sharp conical roofs, only the mosques are realistically depicted. However, the content of particular scenes corresponds to actual historical events in the field. For instance, heavy mortras marked by (12) were brought from Buda after almost one month of delay, and with help of these weapons the fortified (casemated) town gates (16) were penetrated. Panoramas of the fortresses at Zemun and Pančevo are represented in the upper part of the sheet.

The engraving contains a large number of letter and numerical markings, but the legend, i.e. the sheet with explanation of markings disappeared, remaining undiscovered. It probably was not printed at all, because the number of markings is 40.
