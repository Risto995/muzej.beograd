MAP OF THE PRINCIPALITY OF SERBIA

The map is composed according to the best sources of Jovan Bugarski, the former Serbian administrative official and engineer. It was created in Belgrade in 1845. The title is in Serbian language in Cyrillic letters before Vuk's reform. It is geographical - administrative map with: scale in Russian verstas, Turkish geographic miles, table of counties in the Principality of Serbia and their division to districts marked by Latin numbers, with the number of settlements and inhabitants in a district, topographic legend with 28 entries, a dedication of publisher to the Prince Mihailo Obrenović, along with a coloured composition in the lower right corner representing 7 armed men in traditional folk costume with fezzes, standing under the tree with representation of Serbian armour.

The map is monochromatic, relief is hatched, and elevations are not expressed in meters. Apart from the tables with number of villages and inhabitants is the emboss mark of the two authors. The scale is cca. 1 : 371.000.
