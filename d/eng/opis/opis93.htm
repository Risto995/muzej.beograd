PLAN OF BELGRADE

While making a new plan of the Belgrade town and fortress, the publisher and engraver G. Bodenehr has thoroughly used a large combined plan of J. B. Gumpp and M. Wening, published immediately after the conquest of Belgrade in 1688 , drawn by Gumpp on the spot. Several elements and details in the plan imply that Bodenehr used literary sources (perhaps also the pictorial ones) from 1717, in order to publish his plan. Elements from 1717 are: the bridges across Danube and Sava (the latter one is represented as not yet accomplished or demounted); a bridge and embankment over marshlands on the left bank of Sava until Bežanijska kosa; commander's house (G), and a line marked out for construction of the town fortification during Austrian government in Belgrade and Serbia in 1717 - 1739 (F). There is a large number of elements which imply the relations with Gumpp's work, but the most significant one is a lack of Turkish reinforcement of the southern face of fortress by two- horned bastion (Hornwerck), built in 1690-1692 after the plans of Andrej Kornar.

The plan contains an explanation with 18 entries (A - S). Several important locations and objects have to be noted: inner fortification of the Upper town (A); orthodox cemetery and church (Q); open space for communal prayers (O); Turkish cemetery (P); hospital (N), and a mosque adjacent to it (M); han (inn) and bazaar (K and I), and, finally, a palace intended for sojourn of the viziers and the sultan on their arrival to Belgrade (H).
