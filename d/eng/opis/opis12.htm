ALBUM OF THE TREATY OF POŽAREVAC (FIG. 10)

Agha (commander) leading a squad of " delis " (Turkish offensive cavalry), created in the sanjak of Smederevo by Bali-bey. They were famous for their fearlessness. Deli means mad, madly brave in Turkish. Their equipment contained hides of ferocious animals and wings of predatory birds as to give them more terrifying looks. They bear small flags on their spears. In the explication they are identified as madmen (Narren), comedians (Gaukler). It probably is so because the author translated the Turkish word " deli " literally, ignoring the historical context, when relating it to this type of Turkish cavalry. Turkish colour guard then follows consisting of 8 cavalrymen in two rows. Six among them are carrying flags, and two of them are carrying " tugh " (old Turkish banner - a horse tail), signified as "barjak" (identical to "barjak"), i.e., the sacred banner. Colour guard was composed of the Sultan's Court dignitaries. The equerry is behind them followed by 5 horses which are led by two stable-boys. Horses, covered by valuable dressings adorned by gold and silver, are advancing one after another.
