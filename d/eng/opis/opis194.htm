PORTFOLIO OF PICTURES OF THE GREAT ĐORĐE PETROVIĆ KARAĐORĐE (FIG. 9)
KARAĐORĐE'S CONQUEST OF BELGRADE

Karađorđe began the conquest of Belgrade on 30 November (13 December) 1806, the day of St. Andrew. The picture is marked: I Lower town, II Upper town, III Kalemegdan, IV town (varoš), V ditch reinforced by palisades encompassing Belgrade, VI ditches of Prince Eugenee and Fieldmarshall Laudon stretching from Vračar till Sava and Danube .

Schematized and rather inaccurate representation of Belgrade (panorama) along with Serbian insurrectors lined up in six rows to attack. However, components of the picture are correct as they represent (tactical) repetition of the Austrian attack on Belgrade in 1789

Additional legend is below: Serbian batteries at Tašmajdan (a), Vračar (b and v), Savamala (g), Veliko ratno ostrvo (d). Assault on the town: 1. Karađorđe attacks Sava kapija (gate), 2. Knez Sima Marković attacks Varoš kapija, 3. Vasa Čarapić attacks Stambol kapija, 4. Stanoje Glavaš attacks Vidin kapija. There are 20.000 Serbs with 24 cannons, and 16.000 Turks with 280 cannons.
