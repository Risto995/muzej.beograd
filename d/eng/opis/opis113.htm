MILITARY MAP OF FIGHTS AT RAM, KULIČ, SMEDEREVSKA PALANKA AND (BANATSKA) PALANKA

The representations are as follows: Kulič (Kulisch), a fortification on the right bank of Danube across Kovin; Smederevska palanka - Hassan palanka ; Banatska palanka is titled Nova palanka (Ujpalanca) and in the map it is noted as Stara palanka (Vipalanca). The map encompasses the area between Deliblatska peščara (Sand Hugel) and (Banatska) Bela crkva (Weiskirchen) in the north and Smederevska palanka in the south. The conflicts in northeast Serbia in the time of Koča's uprising are represented here. Representation of seizure of the fortified Kulič is in the lower right area, along with an obelisk with laurel wreath inscribed in Latin: "Ad exemplum lib: Bar. Lopresti D.D.D.", meaning: "With public specimen of the book by Bar(tholomeus?) Loprestius D.D.D."

Legend with a number of letter and numerical entries stands below the map saying that the Austrians, under the command of General Harady, Johan Plafy and Nikola Esterhazy, recaptured Stara (Banatska) palanka , and that Smederevska palanka was attacked by the volunteer unit of major Mihaljević (Miholievich). A mark N - 17 is above the upper right corner.
