MANZZINI'S PANORAMA OF BELGRADE

The title is both in German and French language. A view of the fortress and town of Belgrade observed from the ruins of Zemun fortress. The remains of the fortress at Gardo{ in Zemun are represented in the foreground, with a storied wooden watchtower of Austrian army inside it. Several Austrian soldiers, guardians and officers are represented in the tower and in front of it. Two of them are on horses, and one of them observes Belgrade by field glasses, standing on the ruins of the northeastern tower of Zemun fortress. A part of Zemun is seen below them, as well as the anchored ships along the coast at Zemun . The belfry of Nikolajevska church in Zemun is seen to the left of the tower. Central part of the picture is occupied by marshlands around the mouth of Sava, and the Great war island (Veliko ratno ostrvo), divided by a watercourse. Belgrade is depicted in the rear: from left to right, the settlement on the bank of Danube (and /Austrian?/ ships anchored in front of it) is outlined, besides the Lower town surrounded by ramparts, excluding the area around Neboj{a kula (tower) which is surrounded by palisades . Several large (military) buildings are placed in the Lower town, along with a mosque. The Upper town area is densely packed with buildings. The settlement on the Sava terrace fortified by palisades is placed to the far right. The princely armour is placed between German and French title, with a war cross suspended on it. The armour belongs to the prince Charles de Ligne, as it is noted in dedication in French language, in the bottom: Dedicated to His Highness Prince Charles de Ligne, the Cavalier of the War Order of Maria Theresa, the Lieutenant Colonel of the Engineer Corps of His Majesty Joseph, the King of Austria (S. M. J. R. A).
