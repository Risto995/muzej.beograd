ALBUM OF THE TREATY OF PO@AREVAC (FIG. 7)

14 stableboys are leading an equal number of unsaddled horses covered by red velvet dressing with golden embroidery. In front of them rides the main equerry following two messengers of the Court. All of them are preceded by a large emissary echelon in quadruple rows each containing 12 cavalrymen, 2 x 4 infantry soldiers, and finally, symetrically, another 12 cavalrymen. In the lower corner there is an explanation about the echelon represented (in this picture) by 96 cavalrymen and 32 infantrymen, i.e., in effect 1.700 soldiers: hussars ("Hungarian" light cavalry), dragons (medium cavalry) and cuirassiers (heavy, generally armored cavalry) ; in the center, there are representatives of two grenadier regiments (differing by the colour of caps), one of the Prince Alexander, and another of von Geschwind.
