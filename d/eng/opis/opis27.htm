BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 7)
ISOMETRIC PLAN OF COMORRA

Comorra was either the modern Komarom in Hungary, or its twin - Komarno in Slovakia. The town history is placed within the picture. In the upper part there is an explanation of the town location on Danube not far from its confluence with the river Vag. In the lower part is the history which explains that the Emperor Ferdinand I erected the town in XVI century, and that Sinan-pasha besieged it in 1594 (Kodja Sinan pasha had the relics of St. Sava burned). Komarno is represented as a thoroughly fortified town in the shape of an isosceles triangle with its base almost leaning to the riverbank and a point turned toward the Danube coarse.

In the upper right corner is the nuer 37.
