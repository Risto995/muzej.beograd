ROUTE OF THE HABSBURG ARMY FROM FUTOG TO BELGRADE AND THE SIEGE IN 1717

This engraving is a simultaneous representation of map, battle scene and panorama. The route of Imperial army is inscribed between the aggregation place near Futog and Belgrade as the final destination of besiege and conquest. Apart from route, the map shows a scene of the battle of Belgrade and the flight of defeated Turks toward Smederevo . A separate panorama of Belgrade is at its bottom. Its title in Latin language "bellogradum ab Eugeneio capitur" (i.e., Belgrade seized by Eugenee) conceals a chronogram with year of the great victory - 1717 (i.e., MDCLLVVVII). A detail of the advance of Imperial army through marshlands on the left bank of Danube is represented to the right of the panorama of Belgrade. A group of officers is depicted along with several infantrymen - the engineers establishing a passage for the army with bundles of sticks - "fascines". Imperial warship "St. Eugenee" engaged in the besiege of Belgrade is depicted on the unfolded scroll placed in the upper right corner. Imperial armour of Habsburg, double- headed eagle holding the Earth in the right claw, and the Crescent in the left one, is placed in the upper left corner. Below it stands the armour of the Kingdom of Hungary, and the armour of Savoy with Turkish flags is placed below it, right at the bottom. Apart from the noted ones, armours of Slavonia (just above the panorama of Belgrade) and Old Trivalia (above the legend in the lower right corner) are also placed in the engraving.

All explanations are in French language, and at the top center there is a short text related to events which have occurred between June and August 1717 . A large legend is placed at the bottom right, with short explanations about the route, construction of bridges and the battle itself in August 1717 . Apart from the title in Latin, this panorama has its own small legend with 1-6 entries, and another legend with 12-18 entries related to the battle and siege stands below the panorama of Belgrade.
