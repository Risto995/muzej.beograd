PLAN OF BELGRADE FORTIFICATIONS IN 1740

Representation of simplified groundplan of the town of Belgrade, with a ceremony of exchange of Imperial and Turkish emissaries on 10 June 1740. A small panorama (19 x 5,5 cm) of Belgrade viewed from Danube is placed in the upper right corner, and its own legend: A view of the actual fortress of Belgrade. Certain important objects are marked in the Upper and Lower town such as the arsenal (gun-powder depot) (9) and the main guardhouse (7).

The middle part of the plan is occupied by groundplan of the fortified Belgrade, with Nova Srpska savska varoš (Racenstadt, New Serbian town on Sava) outside the walls, on the junction of actual Narodnog fronta St. and Balkanska St. The hospital building and mosque are also placed outside the walls, at the location of the present National Assembly ( Batal-džamija, Batal -mosque? ) and the quarry at Tašmajdan. The objects which were intended for destruction, according to preliminaries of the peace agreement in Belgrade in 1739 , are hatched. Important undamaged objects (constructed during Austrian government in Belgrade and Serbia in 1717-1739) which were given to the Turks, are isometrically represented: barrack, Alexander's (or Wurtemberg) barrack, commander's residence and several Christian temples (Catholic orders - Jesuits, Franciscans, Minorites and White Friars). Barracks, depots and arsenal in the fortress (Upper and Lower town) were also handed over undamaged. Certain events of that period were also depicted, such as the departure of Austrian units and men who have destroyed the fortification, as well as the most important event - celebration and salute of the artillery from Austrian battle ships marking the exchange of emissaries on 10 June 1740.

The new frontier line is fixed along the course of Sava and Danube . A destroyed ditch is visible on the Banatian side, while the ditch on the left bank of Sava, on its mouth, remained undamaged. Simplified cartouche (a column with narrow capital) in the lower right corner, contains a double legend in German language with A - Z (23 entries in total) and with 1 - 14 entries.
