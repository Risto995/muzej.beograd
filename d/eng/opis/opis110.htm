SCENE OF THE CONQUERING OF BELGRADE AND ITS PANORAMA

The engraving is just a part of a large composition describing the conquest of Belgrade in 1688 . The composition is created by Johann Baptist Gumpp who stayed in the headquarters of the besiege army of Maximilian of Bavaria, and the graver was M. Wening. The whole composition was printed in 9 sheets (43,3 x 31,8 cm) arranged in three rows and three columns. Our sheet stands in the upper first row in the first column, i.e., in the upper left corner of the engraving. It represents an attack and seizure of Belgrade by European army. A frieze with two pairs of amorettos with legend (A - O entries in Italian language) between them is placed below the scene. A majority of entries concerns other parts of the engraving .The sheet also contains a large number of numerical entries : 40 - Zemun with fortress; 6 - fortification facing Sava; 4 - so-called Bolma hissar , 1 - fortification of the Upper town of Belgrade fortress, 5 - Zindan gate, and in the the foreground 19 - undestroyed kisok , etc.

Draftsman: Gumpp, Johann Baptista (1651-1728)

Engraver: Wening Michael (1645-1718)

Period: September 1688

Place: Munich

Object: engraving

Material: paper

Size of sheet: 43,3 x 31,8 cm

Legend: Text is in Italian language

Subentries: panorama of Belgrade; the siege of Belgrade in 1688.
