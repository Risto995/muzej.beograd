BATTLE SCENE OF THE CONQUERING OF BELGRADE ON 7 OCTOBER 1789

Representation of the glorious seizure of Belgrade, the town and the main fortress of Serbia, by fieldmarshall Laudon and archduke Ferdinand, seen from Dedinje (Dedinaberg) as it is inscribed in the lower left corner of the picture. The scenes of celebration with food, wine and soldiers' spouses are represented in the foreground. Behind them, the soldiers are guiding the Turkish prisoners (to the left) and baron Laudon (2) and archduke Francis (1) are riding to the right. Central part of the scene is occupied by battle actions, troops lined up in advancement, trenches and approaches, artillery fire, suburbia and fortress in conflagration. Three medallions are placed above the scene. Each lateral medallion is supported by n angel with trumpet, and the middle one is supported by two angels without trumpets. A voice is exclaiming "Vivat" from angelic trumpet on the left, and "Josephus" on the right - "Long live Joseph (II, Emperor and King). In the medallions are represented baron Laudon , Joseph II, and to the far right Francis I, the prince of Koburg. A legend in German language is placed below the picture, with 33 entries. The engraving was probably published in the time of the enthronement of Francis I, the nephew of Emperor Joseph II.
