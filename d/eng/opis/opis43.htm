BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 23)
PLAN OF THE SIEGE OF BELGRADE IN 1717

The plan shows Belgrade and its environs with setting of a battle, i.e., the situation in the end of July 1717 . Wings of the besiege camp of European army are leaning to the rivers and are supplied by fortified bridge accesses. The fortified circumvallation line is facing south with an aim to resist the attack of forces which were sent to unblock Belgrade. Contravallation line opposed to Turkish lines is facing the town. The town line of ramparts was renovated with two horned bastions of the Venetian Andrea Cornar. The army of Savoy was arranged in two lines with artillery between them. At the sides was the light cavalry, dragons and hussars , along with heavy cavalry of cuirassiers and in the center of the setting were infantrymen. Austrian ships pressed Turkish boats and yawls downstream of Zemun , and the besiege artillery, placed on Banatian side, exchanged the barrage with Turkish cannons located in the Lower town (Wasser Stadt). In the upper right corner is an explanation with A - G entries, and on the top of sheet is marked by number 17.
