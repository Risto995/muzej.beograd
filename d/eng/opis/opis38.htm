BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 18)
PANORAMA OF KANJIŽA (CANISHA)

Panorama of Kanjiža in birds-eye view. Turkish graveyard with nishans and several hoisted flags is seen in the front. Town is approached by the levee running above marshlands, then through a gate at the projected redoubt and across the ditch filled with water. The fort has a square ground plan with a bastion at each corner. Three bastions are triangular and the fourth one is septagonal. Behind the fort, across the bridge, one enters a town protected by palisades . Several mosques are dominating both in the fort and in the town. It is noted in the legend, in the lower left corner, that Kanjiža was placed not far from alba Regia (Sekeszfehervar) and the lake of Balaton. The Turks took hold of it in 1600, and after a long blockade it was liberated in 1690.
