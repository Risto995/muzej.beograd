PANORAMIC (ISOMETRIC) PLAN OF THE SIEGE OF BELGRADE IN 1688

A broad panoramic plan of the siege showing a schematic division of Belgrade to the Upper and lower town and the Castle at its center. Besiege troops are arranged around Belgrade, as well as on the left bank of Sava between Ada Ciganlija and its mouth. Suburbia were not depicted but were described textually. From Sava to Danube these are: gypsy settlement, settlement of schismatics - the Orthodox Serbs, Turkish settlement, Catholic part of the town, and finally Jewish suburbs. Scenes of the camp besiege are represented at the bottom of the picture, and the commander of the besiege army Maximilian Emmanuel is represented riding a horse, in the lower left corner. In front of them are echelons of the besiege army with names of commanders. In front of those is a system of excavated approaches leading to ramparts, and a representation of the attack on the main town gate by heavy cannons brought from Buda . A drapery shaped cartouche is placed in the upper right corner, with a legend with 13 entries (A-O).

The title is written in Latin language: Taurunum (traditional Beograd) the gate of Orient - seized on 6 September 1688 by the joint effort of Pope Innocent XI, Austrian and Roman Emperor Leopold the Great, and Elected Prince Maximilian Emmanuel of Bavaria.

The title is erroneous, antique name of Belgrade is Singidunum, and the noted Taurunum is the name of antique Zemun .
