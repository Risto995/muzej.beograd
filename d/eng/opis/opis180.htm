VIEW OF BELGRADE IN 13 PICTURES IN 1869-1882

A large lithography sheet contains 13 views of Belgrade, both general and particular ones. All views are in the shared rim (46,5 x 31,8 cm) and each view has its own rim executed in fine lining. The central, largest one is in larger format (22,3 x 15,1 cm), and the remaining 12 are in the same format (11,1 x 7,3). The central large view contains a legend in Serbian language only, and the remaining views are with legends in Serbian (above the picture) and French and German language (below the picture). An armour of the Principality of Serbia stands between Serbian and German titles Beograd - Belgrad.

01. Central view, a signature The town of Belgrade represents the panorama of Belgrade fortress viewed across Sava. The steamboat - " Semlin " (i.e., Zemun ) moves upstream along with several freighters. Other views, arranged in 4 rows are as follows:

02. Panorama of Belgrade harbour. A steamboat (with unreadable name) pulls the loaded barge in front of it.

03. A view of Old konak (lodge) in Kralja Milana St., demolished after the coup in 1903. The legend "Princely lodge" is in all three above mentioned languages. The building was constructed in 1840, its author is unknown, and it was purchased from Stevan Simić to become the palace of prince Aleksandar Karađorđević.

04. Knez Mihajlova St., at the corner of Vuka Karadžića St. with Delijska česma (fountain) in the center. It was later moved to another location. Legend in three languages - Knjaz Mihajla St. (Prince Mihailo St.)

05. Building of the actual Rectorate of the University at Belgrade, known as Velika škola (High school), but it is best known under the name of Kapetan Mišino zdanje ( Kapetan Miša's edifice) It was designed in 1863 by the architect Jan Nevole , and Miša Anastasijević (Kapetan Miša) donated it to the people of his country. The legend is in three languages - Velika škola (High school).

06. National theater at Trg republike Sq., a work of the architect Aleksandar Bugarski in 1869. The legend is in three languages - Narodno pozorište (National Theater).

07. Hotel Paris, Terazije 27, the work of unknown author. Constructed between 1866 and 1869. Hotel was demolished in 1948 and the ground was cleared for construction of the new building at this place. Legend in three languages: - Gostionica kod Pariza (in Serbien, guest house at Paris), and Hotel Paris (In German and French language).

08 Church of the Holy Assumption (Voznesenska crkva) at the corner of Kneza Miloša St. and Narodnog fronta St. Its design is ascribed to the architect Ristić, and it was constructed in 1863. Legend in three languages: - Voznesenska crkva (Church of the Holy Assumption).

09. Knez Mihajlova 48, Hotel of brothers Krstić, is the work of unknown author in the end of 1860s. It is remarkable that the noted quest house is named "Krsićeva kafana" ("Krstić' cafe") in the large work of Dr. Divna Đurić Zamolo (Hoteli i kafane XIX veka u Beogradu - Hotels and cafes in XIX century Belgrade). Legend: - Gostionica braće Krstić (in Serbien, Guest house of brothers Krstić) and Hotel of brothers Krstić (in French and German language).

10. Military academy, Kneza Miloša St. 29 at the corner with Nemanjina St., a work of the architect Jan Nevole , constructed in 1850. After the project of architect Danilo Vladisavljević, another story was added to the building after the First World War. It was destroyed in the bombardment of Belgrade on 6 April 1941. Legend in al three languages - Vojna akademija (Military Academy).

11. Town hospital in Džordža Vašingtona ST. 14, accomplished in 1868, ascribed to the architect Jovan Frencl. It is known (it is frequently called by that name) Očna klinika (clinic for eyes). It is now the seat of Serbian medical society and the Museum of Serbian medicine. Legend in all three languages: - Varoška (gradska) bolnica (Town hospital).

12. Knez Miloš 's konak (lodge) in Topčider was built in 1834 by the master builder Hadži Nikola Živković . It contains today the permanent exhibition of Historical Museum of Serbia titled "Serbian revolution 1804-1815". Legend in all three languages - Letnjikovac Kneza Miloša u Topčideru (The summer residence of Prince Miloš in Topčider).

13. Vajfert's brewery in Bulevar vojvode Putnika no. 1, built before 1880, the work of unknown author. Today, it is the central section and direction of Beogradska industrija piva (Belgrade brewery). Legend in al three languages: - Parna pivara Đ./orđa/ Vajferta, (Steam brewery of Đ./orđe/ Vajfert).

Apart from this large sheet, Wilhelm Kuen has also published the small album of Belgrade views (in the format 11,1 x 7,3 cm) in hardcover printed in gold titled "Beograd - Belgrad". Central view is in the same format as the others (11,1 x 7,3 cm). However one of the single views - Hotel of brothers Krstić - was left out, probably because of superstition related to number 13.
