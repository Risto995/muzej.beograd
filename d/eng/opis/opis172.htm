TWO SCENES OF THE SIEGE AND CONQUEST OF BELGRADE IN 1688

Two battle scenes are combined in the engraving, placed one above another with a note that lower scene chronologically precedes the upper one. The upper scene represents Belgrade in the moment of final assault of the besiege army and the conquest of town on 6 September 1688 . Operations on the bank of Sava in the level of Ada Ciganlija, construction of bridgehead, and unsuccessful attempt of Turkish army to drive back the besiegers to initial positions on the left bank of Sava are depicted in the lower scene. In the lower left corner of both scenes stands a figure of chief commander Maximilian Emmanuel of Bavaria. The engraving contains 17 letter and numerical entries, but the legend was probably placed in the separate sheet which was lost in the course of time, or it remained in the album (perhaps in a book) from which the engraving has been torn off. Titles were inscribed in ink subsequently for the upper scene: 1688 , conquest of Belgrade by Imperial army under the command of elected prince of Bavaria, and for the lower scene: 1688 . Imperial army's crossing of Sava to Turkish bank. Both texts are in French language.
