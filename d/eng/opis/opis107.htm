PANORAMA AND BATTLE SCENE OF THE CONQUERING OF BELGRADE

The engraving represents a moment when the janissaries of Piri-pasha attack the Lower town from water and take it over, preceding to the conquest of Belgrade. An error was made during wood-cutting, i.e., it was not cut inversely, and thus the mouth of Sava was placed at the east of Belgrade. The same error occurred also in Muenster's work. The Upper town fortifications are marked out, with inner town and the Mill-tower (Muhlturm, Mlinarica) at the mouth of Sava.

Immediately after the fall of Belgrade under Turkish power, the Nuremberg printer Resch published a woodcut which represented the above noted event. These leaflets circulated in Germany and were used in political dispute during an argument between the followers of Reformation and the Catholic reactionaries. As far as we know, there were two different leaflets (although artistically identical). The one that we have is without text, an another one is almost quadrangular in shape with a large text at its top explaining the Turkish danger. This view of Belgrade, the first one which had ever appeared, is well known and for a long time it remained the only one, and thus it served as a model for the famous geographic reference book - Muenster's Cosmographia universalis, printed in Basel for the first time in the mid XVI century.
