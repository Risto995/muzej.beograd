PANORAMIC MAP OF BATTLEFIELD WITH A SCENE OF THE SIEGE OF BELGRADE IN 1688

Representation of the siege and conquest of the town and main fortress of Belgrade, i.e., Greek (orthodox) Weissenburg on 6 September 1688 .

A dense and almost unreadable map encompasses an area between the mouth of Drina and mouth of Sava. The following settlements are noted: Smederevo , Novi grad (? Ujvar), Sremska Mitrovica, Šabac, Kupinovo (Kelpen), Petrovaradin, Zemun and Belgrade (along with other, unidentifiable ones (Romsy, Bokkan, Illesy). Events from the siege of Belgrade are depicted at the bottom left of the engraving: crossing of the army of Emmanuel Maximilian of Bavaria to he right bank of Sava over Ada Ciganlija, besiege, bombardment and conquest of Belgrade. Turkish flight on ships down the Danube is represented in the lower left corner. There are several letter entries on the map, but the legend was either on a separate sheet or on the cut off part.

The title is placed on the flag born by two amorettos (one of them armed). Below it stands a stylization - personified Turkey running away from Europe. Signature of the author is placed upon the white rocks of Petrovaradin, in the lower right corner.
