BATTLE SCENE WITH THE PLAN OF BELGRADE

The town and fortress of Belgrade are represented in the engraving. A battle between Christian and Turkish army at Belgrade on 16 August 1717 is depicted in the foreground. Isometric (spatial) plan of the besiege army camp is represented in the center of the picture, with marked position of units under the command of the archduke of Bavaria, the prince of Portugal (heir to the throne?), count Palfy, and finally the positions of field cannons and heavy mortars . Arrangement of the part of besieging army on the left bank of Sava is represented above the plan (center above). A detail of the battle is depicted below the legend: a moment of decisive assault of the grenadirs , after they have taken over Turkish trenches, when they opened fire and forced the enemy to retreat in disorder. Eugenee of Savoy rides detached, on the left side. A scene of the cavalry assault on Turkish position is placed in the right corner. The cartouche in the upper left corner, in the shape of scroll, contains a short description of events, dedication to Pope Clement XI, and a signature of the author.
