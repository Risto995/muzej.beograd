ALBUM OF THE TREATY OF POŽAREVAC (FIG. 17)

The scenes are separated similarly as in previous picture. Tents and arrangement of people inside and in front of them are almost identical to the previous sheet. Numbers 1, 3, 5, and 7 are on the left, signifying Turkish emissary, interpreter, pashas and aghas, and finally, the janissaries . Presence of the emissaries from Imperial delegation is noted by even numbers 2, 4, 6 and 8: count Oduyer, his interpreter, noblemen and officers and finally, grenadirs standing in the entrance of the tent.

Picture on the right is identical except for numbers 1, 5, 7, and 9, signifying: count Virmont, his interpreter, noblemen and grenadirs (left of the tent entrance); the members of Turkish delegation are noted by numbers 2, 6, 8 and 10: the emissary (titled in the text as Serasker i.e., the commander), his interpreter, pashas, aghas and janissaries (right of the tent's entrance). Numbers 2 and 3 are reserved for the guilded Turkish table covered by cloth and food, as well as the Persian rug.

In the legend for a picture on the left it is noted that the described event (without a date) took place on a location one hour away from the frontier, and that it represented a moment when two emissaries were offered a refreshment.

In the legend for a picture on the right it is noted that it concerns a meeting of count Virmont with the Commander of Niš ( Seraskier von Nissa) one hour away from the frontier, in the moment of refreshment in Turkish tent.
