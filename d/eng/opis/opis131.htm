MAP OF THE BATTLEFIELD AT BELGRADE WITH PANORAMA OF THE TOWN

The plan of Belgrade, capital of Serbia, occupied by Turks in 1739 is represented in this engraving. The title is in French language, placed within a cartouche of column-like form, in the lower left corner. The map is published in 1788, in the very beginning of Austrian- Turkish war but after 24 April 1788 when Šabac was already conquered. Locations of almost every conflict with Turks in the area (since 1441) are noted. The largest amount of data concerns the war and conquest of Belgrade in 1717 . The route of Austrian army from Futog , via Titel and Pančevo to Belgrade is also marked. The pictorial representation of Belgrade could be related to 1717 - a fort with two-horned bastions, i.e., Turkish reinforcements from 1696.

A panorama of Belgrade placed in the upper right corner may also be related to the end of XVIII and the beginning of XVIII century. An Austrian officer and Turkish nobleman are placed next to the cartouche , and below their legs another Turk is represented, his face terrified and his hands folded in Christian mode, for prayer)

All texts are in the French language, and the entire engraving represents typical example of a roundabout propaganda of the war goals. The map is geographically rather imprecise..
