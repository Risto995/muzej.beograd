WEIGEL'S PLAN OF BELGRADE

A situation of the town and its environs and the plan of Belgrade is represented, i.e., the Greek white town, on the banks of Sava and Danube , which was liberated from the Turks with God's help and Christian arms. The title is in German language and it is placed within a horizontal scroll-like cartouche in the upper left corner. Belgrade is represented in this plan along the sharp arch formed by Sava and Danube . The bridges across Sava and Danube (which remained after the siege in 1717 ) are marked by text, as well as the roads to Osijek, Timisoara and Smederevo , the inner fortification of the Upper town (Das Schloss) and, downstream of kula Nebojša (the tower), the Danube harbour in which "the ships could be safely anchored". The plan contains 7 entries (A - G) with legend equivalent to the title, also in the form of scroll, and in German language. The following objects are noted: A) Lower town or Water town, B) "Upper" town enclosed by old wall, i.e., the northwestern zwinger, C) Old and renovated fortress - actually the Upper town, and the attribute "renovated" relates to the attached hornwerk (two-horned bastion) constructed in 1690s after the plans of the Venetian Cornarro, D) northeastern zwinger, E) Serbian town (Srpska varoš, Reittens Stadt - obviously erroneous, it should stand Ratzen - Rasciani, Rascian), F) Pasha's konak (lodge) in the Lower town and G) Eugenee of Savoy 's circumvallation line surrounding the town. The wind rose is to the right. Scale cca 1 : 5.000.
