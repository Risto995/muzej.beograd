ALBUM OF CUNO VON QUITZOW (FRONTISPIECE)

This album is not complete but represents a collection of preserved sheets. The last page or wrapping was not preserved. The author of this album, Cuno von Quitzow, remained unknown to us. Rare occurrence of the album in collections is logical as it has been created at the peak of dynastic arguments in Serbia during mid XIX century. A change in the seat of power and return of the dynasty of Obrenović ( Miloš and Mihajlo) to princely throne occurred within two years after the appearance of the noted album. Members of the dynasty of Karađorđević were banished, their possessions confiscated, and their followers were also in trouble. As far as we know, regarding the size of the album, at least one sheet is missing - A view of the Rakovica monastery, although a larger number of missing sheets should not be excluded.

Album of Belgrade was dedicated to the prince Aleksandar Karađorđević. Text in the frontispiece is in Serbian (left side) with a counterpart in German (right side). "To his Highness Serbian Lord and Prince Aleksandar I Karađorđević dedicated with reverence from Cuno von Quitzow (the name transcribed in German only). An armour of the Principality of Serbia is placed in the center of the sheet, within a white circle with radiating lines.
