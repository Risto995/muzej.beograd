BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 4)
PANORAMA OF BRATISLAVA (PRESBURG)

Panorama of Bratislava as seen from the left Danube bank. The town history represented in a column on the left and half of the column on the right side, contains the names of Bratislava : the Latin one and the Hungarian one - Pojun, and accentuates that it was the capital of upper Hungary, placed 10 miles away from Vienna. During Turkish occupation of Buda , it was the capital of Hungary and its most important economic center at the same time. It is noted that the crown of Hungarian kings was guarded there, and that their coronation was taking place in the church of St. Martin. Above the right upper corner is the number 133.
