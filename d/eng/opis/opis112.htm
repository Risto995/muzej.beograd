MILITARY MAP AND PANORAMA OF KLADOVO

A map of the surrender of Kladovo (Gladova), seized by Imperial-Royal troops under the command of General Fabrius on 09.11. 1789 . The map encompasses the area between Porečka river (Poretz Bach) in the west and Timok in the east. The upper, northern border of the map is represented by the course of Danube . A route of Austrian army from Kovilovo to Kladovo is shown, along with the siege of Kladovo and the march of one unit to Kusjak (on the bank of Timok), along the road to Vidin. A scene of surrender is below the map - a group of five Turkish dignitaries offer the keys of the town of Kladovo to General Fabrius and his officers. A panorama of fortified Kladovo is in the rear with troops lined up below the town walls. A short text in German below the picture describes the siege and capture of Kladovo , as well as the booty of 31 cannons and 2544 centners of gun- powder. A mark of the enumeration of illustration is above the upper right corner - NČ 50, implying that, apart from the format, it was torn out from a book.
