ALBUM OF THE TREATY OF POŽAREVAC (FIG. 9)

Frontal part of the escort of Turkish delegation: the first echelon are cavalrymen ( spahis ). In total 1.700 soldiers. There are 96 spahis represented in two groups of quadruple rows with 4 men in each of them. There are 32 infantrymen (corresponding to the echalone of representatives of the Imperial army in the picture). A group of janissaries then follows with a commander at its front and in its rear. They differ from the previous group in that they are, apart from guns, equiped by sabers as well, and are signified as "Defeckerisch" - a term which is almost untranslatable. The last echelon represents a group of 24 cavalrymen in three rows bearing small flags on their spears, led by the agha. In the right bottom there are 6 small cannons with two attendants, which saluted a welcome to the Imperial delegation.
