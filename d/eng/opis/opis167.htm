ILLUSTRATED PROPAGANDA LEAFLET (WITH PANORAMA OF BELGRADE)

A panorama of Belgrade (size of the picture 31,5 x 19,8 cm) viewed from the southern, mainland side. All parts of the town are depicted, although the author (Sedlmayer) did not possess the pictorial scheme, and hence, guided by literary description, he placed the town (lower town, i.e., in German texts Water town - Wasser Stadt) erroneously into southern part of the town, along with pasha's konak (lodge) (4). Depictions are as follows: Zemun - "Semblin" (11), Pančevo (10), pontoon bridges across Sava and Danube (9 and 8), large Imperial battleship - river corvette (8) and Turkish yawls blocked in the narrow part of Danube below the fortress (12). Legend in German language with 1-12 entries is placed in the lower right corner. A thorough geographical- historical text about Belgrade stands below the picture. The author made an error in listing because the Hungarian name of Belgrade was printed "Standoralben" instead of Nandor Alba (the name of Belgrade in Hungarian). As regards the ancient history, he identified the town as a powerful fortress and mentions the Serbian (Reissischen) King Stefan Dragutin or Despot Stefan Lazarević who have fortified it. Subsequently he deals with glorious defenses of Belgrade during Turkish aggressions in 1440, 1456, 1494, and finally he notes the siege and conquest in 1521 by Suleiman the Magnificent. Afterwards, he mentions that the army under the command of Maximilian of Bavaria seized Belgrade on 6 September 1688 during the reign of Leopold I, but that the town was taken over by Turks once more on 8 October 1690.

The final part of the text notes that the sovereign Emperor Charles VI and his army under the command of general-lieutenant prince Eugenee of Savoy began the siege on 18 June 1717 ( the year is not marked). Obviously, this leaflet was published before the fall, or the news about the fall of Belgrade on 17 August 1717 . Data about the author are below the text.
