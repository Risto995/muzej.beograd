BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 20)
GEOGRAPHIC MAP OF THE AREA BETWEEN BELGRADE, PANČEVO, ZRENJANIN AND NOVI SAD

The map shows a network of rivers encompassing the lower course of Tisza, Sava, Begej, and Tamiš as well as the Danube course between Novi Sad and Belgrade. Srem is marked as Sclavonia pars (part of Slavonia), Bačka as a part of Hungary (Hungaria pars) and Banat as the Timisoara side (Themeswarer seiten). In the upper right corner, the number 32 is marked along with a wind rose orienting the map. On the right in the center is plan of the siege of Belgrade showing contravallation line ( towards oncoming Turkish army) and circumvallation line (toward besieged Belgrade) of Eugene of Savoy 's fortified camp. In the lower right corner is a provisional title of the map and explanation with entries A - E.
