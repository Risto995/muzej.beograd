SPAAR'S ALBUM - "NOVA ISKRA" COPY (FIG. 1)
A VIEW OF BELGRADE, THE TOWN AND GERMAN TOWN, FROM ZEMUN

Panorama of the town viewed from the Zemun fortress, parts of Zemun , and a plateau between Bežanijska kosa and Sava. In the center of picture is the Belgrade fortress with clearly marked Sahat kula (Clock-tower), poorly visible Sava terrace with Srpska varoš (Serbian town) and modern Dorćol - the German settlement in Belgrade during Austrian government 1717 - 1739 . The engraving is a copy from Spaar's album, published as an illustrated supplement of "Nova iskra". "Nova iskra" is illustrated magazine published in Belgrade since 1899 to1907, by Rista Odavić, Serbian writer, a public personality engaged in culture and politics. Printed by lithography workshop of P. Marković - Belgrade.
