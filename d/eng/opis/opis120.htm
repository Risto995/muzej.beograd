PLAN OF CONSTATINOPLE AND PANORAMAS OF 6 TOWNS IN CENTRAL AND SOUTHEAST EUROPE

A sheet with frontispiece vignette of the book, plan and the panoramas was probably intended as illustration for some book

A1 frontispiece vignette - 12,3 x 11,4 cm, with a small map of the upper course of Danube and a title in German "New enlarged edition of Danubian coasts".

A2 Plan of Constantinople - 12,4 x 11,4 cm, with 8 entries in German language. The notorious Turkish prison of Seven towers (5) is marked among other objects. Number 114 is placed in the upper right corner.

B 1 Munkačevo (Mongatz in Slovakia) - 11,4 x 7 cm, a town on the plateau of high steep rock represented in the moment of siege and bombardment. There are 13 letter entries (A - N) without legend. Number 298 is placed in the upper right corner.

B 2 Erlau (Erla) - 11,4 x 6,1 cm, with 5 entries and legend in German language. Number 78 is placed in the upper right corner.

C 1 Belgrade - 11,3 x 6,1 cm, a view across the Danube to the harbour, Lower town, Upper town ad the mouth of Sava. It contains a legend in German language and 5 entries. Number 94 is in the upper right corner.

C 2 Šabac (Sabaz) - 11,4 x 6,1 cm, a panorama viewed from he south. The settlement and fortress are isolated, surrounded by water and the mouth of Bosut is placed erroneously downstream of Šabac. There is a legend in German language with 4 entries. Number 93 is placed in the upper right corner.

D 1 Sisak (Sissek) - 11,3 x 6 cm, fortified town at the mouth of Kupa and Oder (!) (conceivably Ukrina) to Sava. It has 4 entries and a legend in German language. Number 90 is in the upper right corner.

D 2 Edirne (Adrianopel) - 11,4 x 6,1 cm, a view of the town with large number of minarets and a large bridge across Maritsa. Number 112 is placed in the upper right corner.

Period: beginning of XVII century

Object: engraving

Tehnique: copper engraving

Material: paper

Size of sheet: 27,6 x 36,6 cm

Legend: Text is in German language

Entries: plan ; panorama

Subentries: plan of Constantinople; panoramas of the towns
