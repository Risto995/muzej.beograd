PLAN OF BELGRADE OR ALBA GRAECA FROM 1717

The title in Latin language is noted in a cartouche in the upper right corner - the true and new description from 1717. The plan is created before the Austrian reconstruction began, though with circumvallation line (H) marked out around the town. The Lower town - "Water town", Vodena Varo{ (A) is noted, along with the Upper town or interior settlement, encircled by ancient ramparts (B). In fact it was the fortification renovated during Turkish period, i.e., Bolma hissar (bol - in Turkish means large, abundant), the interior fortification of the Upper town - the castle (C), the Lower town described as "an old but renovated fortification" (D), the commander's residence in the Lower town (G), Srpska varo{ (Serbian town, "Retzen Stadt, "La Citta delli Rasciani", "La ville Rasciens", "Civitas Rascianorum") (F). Latin designations are marked directly in the plan: the road to Osijek, the rivers of Sava and Danube , the Danube harbour along with the Lower town - "Aquoeductus", and finally the road and bridge to Timisoara. A legend with 8 entries (A - H) in Latin language is in the upper left corner, formed as a vertical scroll. A cartouche in the upper right corner is decorated by symbols of war - drums and banners, Turkish on the left and Austrian on the right

A woodcut is added below the plan - a translation of the legend into German, Italian, and French language, and at the same time the translation of all texts in the plan. Rather unusual translation into three living languages was probably represented in order to increase the selling of this plan. Although the plan is dated with 1717, the town destructions were not depicted and thus it may be assumed that the situation which immediately preceded the war was represented, apart from the besiege line traced by Eugenee of Savoy . The scale in common (traditional) fathoms is given next to the legend in the upper part of the plan, and assuming that "common" fathom was 1,896 m, the scale was cca 1 : 2.462.
