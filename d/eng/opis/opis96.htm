PANORAMA OF BELGRADE

A southern, downstream view of Belgrade, with complete representation of Savska padina (terrace of the Sava river). The lowland sight of the town is marked by several important, large buildings, and flags fluttering above the town. To the right of the center are: Saborna (cathedral) church, Turkish flag on Belgrade fortress close to Sahat kula (Clock- tower) and four flags (Russian, French, Italian and unidentified one). To the left is Zemun , a part of Veliko Ratno ostrvo (Great War island), and Austrian frontier guardhouse right at the mouth of Sava. Author's signature is in the lower right corner. Below, outside the picture is the title Belgrade (in German language and in Serbian language as it was spelled prior to Vuk's reform). Below to the left is author's name written in Cyrillic, and to the right is the name of publisher, also in Cyrillic letters (Reifenstein and Resch - Vienna).
