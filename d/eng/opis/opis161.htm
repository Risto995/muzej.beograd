PLAN OF BELGRADE AFTER THE ACCOMPLISHMENT OF AUSTRIAN RECONSTRUCTION WITH A SEPARATE SCENE OF TURKISH SIEGE OF THE TOWN IN 1739

Representation of the siege of Belgrade in 1739 in the upper left corner, with explanation that the great vizier besieged it with 90.000 men, 200 cannons and 30 mortars . Below the explanation is a legend with 1 - 8 entries, describing the positions of cannon and mortar batteries, Christian lines of defense, along with Turkish contravallation and circumvallation lines with a camp between them. The plan with a wind rose in the lower left corner, represents Belgrade and fortress after the reconstruction accomplished by Habsburgians according to the finest system of fortification - that of the French Marshall Vauban (1633-1707). Below the wind rose is a short explanation noting that after taking the fortress over from Turks on 30 August 1739 , it has been demolished by the Christian army (an that is partially correct). A legend with A - Z (24 marks in total) and 1- 12 entries in German language is placed in the lower right corner. All important objects in he town and fortress are represented. Bastions along the town line of defense are marked by numbers 2 - 8 (in the same order as follows: St. Mary, St. Elisabeth, St. Eugenee, St. Benedict , St. Charles, St. Joseph and St. Leopold). The large (Alexander's or Wurtemberg's ) barrack is marked by letter "S", and by number 12 with dots are marked parts of the town which were burned during the attack of enemy. Veliko ratno ostrvo (Great War island) is erroneously marked as Ada Ciganlija (Zigeuner Insul). A figure of the god Mars stands above the legend, with flags behind it.
