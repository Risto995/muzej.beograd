TWO PICTURES FROM THE SIEGE OF BELGRADE IN 1688

Representation of the appearance of glorious town and the main crossing of Greek (orthodox) Weissenburg, i.e., Belgrade.

The engraving is divided in two separate, thematically related parts. The upper, slightly smaller part represents the panorama of Belgrade, viewed from the north, across Danube , with the arrangement of besiege army around town. The following objects are clearly marked in Belgrade, the Upper and Lower town: inner fortification, entrance to the harbour, watermill on Sava with pontoon bridge identified as the Turkish one (?). A cartouche (in the shape of laurel wreath) with dedication to Maximilian of Bavaria, with entire citation of his reigning title, is placed in the upper right part. An identical cartouche is placed in the left corner with image of Maximilian of Bavaria (Dr. Rajko Veselinović stated that it represented an image of the Emperor Leopold I Habsburg, but it is not at all in accord with the text of cartouche on the right, and the image itself corresponds more to the archduke of Bavaria). The wind rose is placed in the lower left corner, and the schematic plan of Belgrade fortress titled "Belgrad Ichonographice" is on the right, within a separate square (6,3 x 6,3 cm), the Upper town is marked as Castle (Schloss).

Lower scene represents the panorama of Belgrade viewed from the southern, mainland side, along with arrangement of infantry and cavalry (with names of the commanders) and the moment of decisive assault for the seizure of the town on 6 September 1688 . A legend with 11 letter entries (A-L) in German language is placed in both lower corners.
