BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 22)
PANORAMA OF BELGRADE (GRIECHISCHWEISSENBURG)

A large ("flaglike") panorama of Belgrade as seen from the Veliko ratno ostrvo (Great war island). The preserved part of the panorama shows Danubian part of Belgrade with the tower of Nebojša, an already buried Danube harbour and a part of the Upper town. There is a pontoon bridge across Danube upstream of the town. Adjacent to the torn down part of the sheet, there are two Danubian mills (" prekaja "). Below the panorama is an explication with 6 entries, followed by a short history and description of the town (although partial because it was torn down).Hence, the remaining part of explanation should have been placed on the right (torn off) side. It is noteworthy that it represents one of the most popular panoramas of Belgrade, which have been published very frequently, and its prototype was a view of Belgrade by Michael Vening with Bodehner's redaction. According to the preserved part of town description and history, its names could be reconstructed as follows: Alba Graeca (in Latin), as well as Taurunum (wrong! It is the Roman name of Zemun ), Nandor Alba (in Hungarian). It is noted that the town was besieged in 1440, 1450, 1493, 1494, and in 1521 when it was finally taken (in August). The army of the Bavarian prince Maximillian Emanuel (the commander) occupied it in 1688 , but the Turks besieged it and took it over in 1693. Finally, the town was besieged by the army commanded by Eugene of Savoy , and after a decisive battle with Turkish army which arrived to help the besieged town, it was occupied by Christians on 19 August 1717 .
