BELGRADE - SAVA AND DANUBE CONFLUENCE

Panorama of Belgrade at the Sava and Danube confluence. Belgrade is represented from the southern, mainland side. However, the draftsman did not have a good quality model, and worked on the basis of inadequate literary description. Therefore, he moved the whole Lower town (placed on the banks of two rivers and protected in its rear by the mass of Kalemegdan, i.e., the Upper town), to the south side, and it hence appears as the southern suburbia of the fort. Inner fortification (1) is marked as a single unit, and it should have been represented as a part of the Upper town. The konak (lodge) of pasha is in the Lower town (4). Presentation of two-horned bastions (initially constructed by the Turks after the project of a Venetian - Andrea Cornarro) is significant. A mark "Fig. XI" stands in the upper left corner, implying without doubt that it is a part of an album or atlas.
