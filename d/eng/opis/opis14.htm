ALBUM OF THE TREATY OF POŽAREVAC (FIG. 12)

In the front is salma-agha (the high emissary beylerbey 's gunsmith), followed by kapichilar-agha (commander of the Court guard), and by two learned men, the gentlemen responsible for German (Teutsch) questions. Behind them is a group of 8 armed infantrymen signified as "Crattin" (the meaning remained unresolved). The center of the scene is represented by a large infantry group with a cavalryman in its center. The cavalryman is Ibrahim-pasha, beylerbey of Rumelia (European Turkey). He is attributed as an executive official, titular ("Dittular"). Close to him are two meteris-bashas, responsible for the water supply, "Mataricks". Close to them are two doveji-bashas (dova - prayer), responsible for religion rituals. All of the noted ones are surrounded by 30 servants armed with spears, ("Cruhatars" - ?). Behind them are riding beylerbey 's treasurer, probably a gunsmith at the same time ("Silighar" and "Hasnatur"), the keeper of treasury books ("Hasnakatipi") and the turnkey of treasury. At the rear are 8 ichoghlans on horses, the Court attendants which otherwise belong to the janissary corps.
