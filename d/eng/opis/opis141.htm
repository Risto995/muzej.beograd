PLAN AND PANORAMA OF BELGRADE AFTER SEUTTER

Belgrade or Greek Belgrade (Weissenburg) situated between the rivers of Sava and Danube , called the lock opener of Hungary. The title in Latin and German language is placed within shell-like cartouche in the upper left corner of the plan. The whole sheet is a kind of compilation accomplished in large format. The upper coloured part is occupied by plan (A), and the lower non-coloured part is the panorama of Belgrade (B).

A) Belgrade during Austrian government (1717-1739) is shown in the plan. A delineation of new fortification constructed by the Austrians during their government in Belgrade and northern Serbia is marked out. At the same time, new fortifications around Kalemegdan (Belgrade fortress) are outlined (although rather inaccurately), though the fortress representation corresponds to its appearance during the siege of 1717 . A large cartouche with history of Belgrade is placed in the lower right corner. It notifies that Plinius has already noted it as (Latin) Taurunum , that Turkish Tzar Murat (Amurat) besieged it unsuccessfully in 1440, and that the siege under the command of his son ( Mohammed the Conqueror), who besieged the town with150.000 men, was equally unsuccessful. Suleiman I seized it in 1521 , and the town remained in Turkish hands until the fortunate siege when it was taken over by Imperial forces under the command of Maximilian Emanuel of Bavaria. Finally, after the wars terminated by treaty of Po`arevac, it was given to the (German).Emperor. Construction of the Capuchin monastery in 1735 is noted as the last event in the noted history. Legend with 27 entries (A - Z and a - e) is placed in the left corner, opposed to historical notes. It relates to the explanation of particular areas of the town, and more important old and new constructed objects. Historical notes and legend are both in German language. In the bottom of the plan is the scale in fathoms (Toisen), 100 fathoms are 2,8 cm, and as one fathom is equal to 1,896 m or 1,949 m, the scale is cca between 1 : 6771 and 1 : 6960. The plan is 35,3 cm large (height).

B) Panorama of Belgrade, titled at its top: Belgrade or Greek White town (Weissenburg). Both upper corners are occupied by legends with 7 entries in total (a - g). The work is a copy of Bodenehr's (Gabriel Bodenehr) panorama of Belgrade.
