MILITARY MAP WITH PANORAMA OF FORTIFIED SOKOL NA DRINI

The map represents seizure of Lešnica (Leseniza), Lipnica (Lippaniza) and Loznica (Losenitza) - toward Zvornik and Sokol (Zokol) by Imperial-Royal troops under the command of General Chernel on 22. 10. 1789.

The map encompasses the Lower Drina valley and represents the route of Austrian troops from Šabac, via Valjevo, consisting of 5.000 men under the command of General Chernel, and simultaneous marching of two battalions of infantry and division cavalry ( hussars ) under the command of colonel Davidović via Lešnica and Lipnica, as well as the attack on the pasha of Zvornik and his forces at Loznica. A panorama of Sokol is in the lower right corner, along with representation of Austrian army during march. A legend in German language is below the map, and number 55 is marked above the right corner (implying that the sheet was torn out from some book).
