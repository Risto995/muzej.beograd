VIEW OF BELGRADE IN 1849

A view of Belgrade from the southern, mainland side. Among many small family houses there are several large, European looking storied houses. In the center, slightly to the left is the barrack with lined up soldiers. Among other objects one may note Saborna crkva (Cathedral church), and Patriarchy across it ( Metropolitan palace) with a cross above its entrance. To the right of the church is Old building (probably), the two-storied building with two naves in Kralja Petra St. (demolished in 1938). In the foreground are several men and women clad in "Serbian town dressing", a Turkish rider, and to the far right are two Serbian officers riding and wearing high fur hats ("čak") decorated by tufts. Zemun is perceptible in the background of Sava, downstream to the far left, as well as the frontier guardhouses on the mouth of Sava.
