MILITARY MAP WITH PANORAMA OF BELGRADE AND ITS SURROUNDINGS

This draft represents the surroundings of Belgrade and Zemun with confluence of Tisa, Begej, Tamiš and Sava to Danube , along with adjacent channels such as Ervenica, Kustoš, Karaš, Dunavac Fizel etc., on 28 July 1788.

The map is just a schematic representation of a battle scene, and with regards to the fact that it was drawn and signed by an "Imperial officer", it may not be thoroughly precise. For example, Višnjica (Visnitza) is placed to the east of Belgrade, in the hilly area far off the Danube ; Taurunum , the antique name for Zemun , is placed near Ruma; the confluence of Bosut and Sava is placed to the south, downstream of Šabac, etc. However, the map is splendidly accomplished. At the top right stands an eagle with thunderbolts, a symbol of war and victory. At the left bottom, below the scale, the panorama of Belgrade (its appearance in mid XVII century) is struck by thunderbolts thrown by an eagle which stands on the pile of Turkish corpses. Mars, the god, surrounded by Turkish banners and thugs, stands on the pile of weapons opposite to the eagle, carrying the shield (with Imperial armour depicted on it!) and a sword. In the corner at the far right is a cartouche , with turban above it, which holds a legend for panorama with 5 entries in German language. The wind rose is placed adjacent to the cartouche . The cardinal points are rotated for 180 degrees, and thus they appear outside of the map border. Cardinal points are additionally denoted in Latin. A portrait of the sultan Abdul Hamid I, with a note that he was born in 1726 (A. H. ruled between 1774 and 1789).

Draftsman: unknown Imperial officer
