PLAN OF BELGRADE

The plan represents an action of Imperial army during famous campaign in 1717 until the conquest of Belgrade, according to information of (unnamed) big General.

Plan of war operations in the summer 1717 was accomplished by the conquest of Belgrade. The plan consisted of three unequal parts: 1. Central part of the plan, 2. Plan of Turkish attack on positions of the Eugene of Savoy 's army (in the upper right corner) and 3. Plan of the battle at Belgrade with specified explanation (in the lower left corner).

1. Central part of the plan: It begins in the upper left corner with a plan of the Petrovaradin fortress and the position of military camp near Futog. The initial position of Haubstein corps is indicated (erroneously inscribed as "Howitzer" - "Haubitschen" corps), and its advance along the right bank of Danube between 23 and 26 June to Zemun (passing by Beška, Karlovci and Slankamen), as well as its battle positions on the left bank of Sava (between the river and Bežanijska kosa). The main forces advanced along the left bank of Danube , across the marshlands, Tisa, Tamiš and Danube branches, and arrived to the Danube shore upstream of its confluence with Tamiš, near Belgrade. In the center of this plan is the plan of Belgrade with position of Turkish garrison, consisting of 40 000 men. Next to it is fortified Austrian camp between fortified lines ( contravallation line toward town and circumvallation one toward south), but also between Turkish garrison and the large Turkish army led by great vizier . The Turks arrived to Belgrade on 29 July and have organized a camp with left wing leaning to Sava and the right wing to Danube trying to cross it and push the Austrians from their positions in Banat. Plan of Belgrade is schematically represented, but it is remarkable that eastern, entirely completed two-horned bastion (Hornwerck) is shown. Topographic explanation belongs to this part of plan (in the lower right corner) with specific designations for grenadier company, infantry battalion, dragon regiment, cuirassier regiment, hussar regiment and Turkish formations (ordia) of spahis , i.e., the royal and feudal cavalry and (ordia) of janissary infantrymen. In the center, on the left, is a large explanation placed below a battle eagle with a sword in the left claw, and a crescent and Turkish tuft (decoration on turban) in the right claw. Explanation with 22 letter entries (A - Z) and 16 numerical entries.

2. Plan of Turkish attack to circumvallation line across the lower course of Topčiderka and Mokroluški potok (?).

3. Plan of the battle of Belgrade with special explanation containing 11 letter entries (A - L).
