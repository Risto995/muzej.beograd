THE ZEMUN HARBOUR AND PANORAMA OF BELGRADE

In the foreground of lithograph is a genre scene with a group of people clad in oriental and traditional clothes standing on the coast. Behind them are two water bearers (sakadžija) standing in shallow water and replenishing the barrels (sak) with it, as well as several women with shoulder poles on which pails are hung. Behind all of them are several barges and large boats, and in the background, in the center of the left side is the panorama of Belgrade fortress (northeastern rampart), and large military buildings in the Lower town below it. Immediately below the picture on the left: "gez. V. J. Alt", and on the right "Druck v. Kunike". Below the picture in German language: Srem and Turkey, a view from Zemun to Belgrade, and below it (subsequently inscribed) 1820. The sheet comes from the work "two hundred and sixty four panoramas along the Danube from its spring to its mouth in the Black Sea" (Zweihundert vier und sechzig Donau - Aussichten nach dem Laufe des Donaustromes von seinem Ursprung bis zu seinem Ausflusse in das Schwarze Meer). The book with comments of Georg Rumi was published in Vienna by Adolph Kunike. Georg Karl Boromau Rumi, a Slovak, was headmaster of the Lyceum at Karlovci in 1820s, according to Prof. Medaković.
