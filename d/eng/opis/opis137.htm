BATTLE SCENE NEAR BELGRADE ON 16 AUGUST 1717 WITH PANORAMA OF THE TOWN

Representation of the battle between Turkish and Christian cavalry is in the foreground, and to the far left rides the prince Eugenee of Savoy (1) with an eagle of victory above his head. A soldier with seized Turkish thug is behind him. Panorama of Belgrade is seen above Savoy (view from Vračar) along with arrangement of Turkish crew in the Upper and Lower town. The suburbs, with residence of the commander-in-chief (6), and Turkish cemetery above it on the spot of actual Tašmajdan (4) are placed between the town and besieging army (5). The exit of Austrian troops from the camp and battle with Turks (7), positions of Turkish batteries (10) and cavalry (11), and finally, the Turkish camp (8) with residence of the grand vizier - commander (serasker) of the army coming to help the besieged Belgrade (9), are represented on the right side. The scene is provided with legend in Italian language with 13 (1-13) entries.
