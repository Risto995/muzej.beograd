PANORAMA OF BELGRADE, CAPITAL OF THE KINGDOM OF SERBIA

A view of Belgrade from the hill (located at Gardo{ - Be`anijska kosa?) as if it were a bird's eye view. Four people wearing European clothes are in the foreground, and the left bank of Sava with Belgrade across it, is below them. A small watercourse is represented close to the mouth of Sava (probably an error), and the panorama of Belgrade is in the rear of above noted depictions. The town is divided into the fortress and suburbs with a vacant area (an area on the ridge of Kalemegdan Terazije , still with no buildings and settlement). Three water mills ( prekaja ) are placed on the bank of Sava. Seven ships, and a large sail-boat among them, are depicted on Danube toward the harbour. A short historical- geographical description of Belgrade in Italian language is situated below the title. Its position (40 degrees and 40 minutes of the northern latitude and 39 degrees and 51 minutes of the eastern longitude) along with short text about the conquest in 1717 and significance of that conquest (for Catholic world) are also noted.
