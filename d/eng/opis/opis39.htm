BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 19)
PANORAMA OF ALBA REGIA (STULWEISSENBURG)

The panorama represents Sekeszfehervar (Alba Regia) as a town consisting of several parts. Four parts of the town are placed on fortified river islands, along with two fortified suburbia on the coast. Communications with town and among its parts are represented by eight bridges. Parts of the town placed on the islands are packed with large, solid buildings, mainly mosques and churches, while the suburbia on the coast are dominated by two mosques and one multistoreyed tower. Notes about town are on the left side. It is explained that the town lays upon the river of Sarva (Sarowitz) which forms a triangle with other two rivers. According to the description, the town has five suburbia, three outer and two inner ones. Since ancient times the Hungarian kings were crowned there in a church which has no counterpart in the rest of Hungary.Turks occupied the town in 1543, and Christians took it over again in 1688 . In the meantime, since Septemer 1601 until August in the next year, it has been in the possession of Habsburgs. Nimber 165 is marked on the right, above the panorama.
