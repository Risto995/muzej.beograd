THE TURKS ABANDONING BELGRADE ON 10 OCTOBER 1789

Depiction of events from 10 October 1789 , two days after the surrender when the Turks - crew from the fortress and civilian population - abandoned Belgrade. Fieldmarshall Gideon Laudon (1), represented with his officers (5), is supervising the act of capitulation. A group of Turkish officials (3), followed by the commander (2) of defeated crew of the fortress, is passing by Laudon and military unit (6) ready to occupy the fortress. The legend in French (to the left) and German (to the right) clarifies the scene and notes the data, probably exaggerated, about the booty. Number 56 is marked above the upper right corner, implying that the sheet was part of an album about the wars of Francis II (i.e., Francis I, the Emperor of Austria since 1806).
