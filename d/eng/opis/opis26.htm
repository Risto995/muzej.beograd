BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 6)
PLAN OF THE KLOSTERNEUBURG FORT

Plan of the fortified town Neuheusel (Vywar in Hungarian), i.e., modern Klosterneuburg or Nove Zamky on Nitra (Slovakia). The modern Hungarian name of town is Ersekujvar. According to the plan, the town had a shape of regular hexagon, with a fortified bastion at each angle, an orthogonal street network and a square at its center. In the upper right corner is the number 127, and in the lower one stands a scale in Viennese ells (100 ells = 3,8 cm). In the lower left corner is the section with a scale expressed in building feet (60 feet = 2,9 cm).

The town is surrounded by water and could be entered by two gates placed at its opposite ends, with bridges located at the north and at the south (if the orientaion of plan was accurate). Outside of the plan, both on its left and right border, the town history is inscribed. It says, that Bocskay conquered the town with Turkish aid in 1605, and that the Imperial forces brought it back in 1607. The Turks took it over also in 1663, but since 1710 the town was finally in the control of Habsburgs.
