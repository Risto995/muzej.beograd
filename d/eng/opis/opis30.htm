BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 10)
PANORAMA OF VELIKI VARADIN (GROSWARDEIN)

Panorama of the town surrounded by water with partially damaged town walls, reinforced by several towers. The town is approached by a bridge fortified by gate. At the hill, right above the bridge, stands a fortress in the shape of regular hexagon with a castle in its center. Settlements are scattered around the town, across the river. A settlement represented in the front bottom of the picture, has a small palisades fortification. Text in the lower right corner notes that it was a famous town on the river of Karas in Transylvania besieged by Turks for the first time in 1598, and that it remained in Turkish possession between 1660 and 1662. This town, called Veliki Varadin by Serbs, Grosswardein in German, Nagy Varad in Hungarian, is actually the Romanian town on Karas called Oradea or Oradea Mare.

In the upper right corner is the number 76.
