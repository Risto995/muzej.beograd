BODENEHR'S ALBUM

The founder of the family firm is Hans Georg Bodenehr (1631-1704). He started the business in Augsbourg in 1677 with publication of small geographical atlases. Later, he continued the business with three sons: Gabriel, Moritz (died in 1749) and Georg Conrad (no data about him). In all probability, Gabriel moved the business to Vienna at a certain moment. An important endeavor was the publishing of "Curiosity atlas" (Atlas curieux) in 1704-1737.

The album represented here is an unusual compilation of various pictures from atlas along with a number of maps. Judging by these facts, as well by fashion and spirit of the epoch, it probably represents a part of the large edition of "Force d'Europe".

Author: Bodenehr Gabriel, (1664-1758)

Period: 1699-1720

Place: Augsbourg

Object: album

Tehnique: copper engraving , a part of it is coloured

Material: cardboard

Size of sheet: 32,5 x 19,3 cm

Watermark: On some of the sheets it is present and on the others not. Two types of mark are represented: stylized scorpio and an elongated oval with crowned human figure in the center. Above it is an apple with symetrical "Greek" cross at the top. Along the sides of the oval there are other stylizations as well.

Legend: Text is in German language.
