PANORAMA OF BELGRADE AND A BATTLE SCENE FROM 1788

A view of Belgrade and a part of Royal Imperial army at Zemun in 1788. In the front, on the left are the remains of Medieval fortification at Gardoš, and the already formed settlement of Zemun below it. Battle ships are anchored along the coastline at Zemun . In the front, in the right corner is the emperor Joseph II with his headquarters. Behind him are the echelons of soldiers and the representation of clash with Turks on the left bank of Sava, in the level of Ada Ciganlija. In the rear is the panorama of Belgrade under artillery fire, and in its interior are: the settlement on Danube terrace (2), Upper town of the Belgrade fortress (1), Lower town (3), and Nova srpska varoš (New Serbian town) beyond fortified town (5). The panorama is furnished with explanation with 14 numerical entries in German language. Entry no. 9 is erroneous because one of the Danube branches is marked as "Sebeš" - a branch if Tisa. Ouside of the scene, in the upper right corner is number 8 implying that the sheet belonged to an album about the siege of Belgrade, or about Austrian-Turkish conflicts.
