PLAN OF AUSTRIAN RECONSTRUCTION AND BELGRADE FORTIFICATIONS IN 1717-1739

Austrian plans of the reinforcement of Belgrade fortress, according to the best contemporary fortification system of the French Marshall Vauban. On the right side of the plan is the cartouche with German Imperial eagle at its top (black double-headed crowned eagle with wings spread out, with sword in the right, and Imperial scepter in the left claw) containing the town history until 1717. Legend with 24 entries (A - Z) is below it and right at the bottom is the scale in fathoms ( klafter ). The markings are as follows: the town (A), Lower town - Vodena varoš (B), German town on the terrace of Danube (C), Upper town of Belgrade fortress, noted as the Castle (D), the gates of Šabac, Wurtemberg, the Emperor, Timisoara, Petrovaradin (in the same order E, F, G, H, and I), the Jesuit church (K), the Trinity church (L), the Capuchin church (M) and the Franciscan church (N), commander's residence (O), Serbian Metropolis on the terrace of Sava - at the spot of actual Patriarchy of the Serbian Orthodox church (Q), redoubts (T), water ditches (W), Serbian settlement outside of the town on the terrace of Sava (U), etc.

A note in ink is written above the depiction, in bad Latin, obviously after the Turkish conquest of Belgrade in 1739 : "Factus est Dominus vetus Inimicus: proecipitavit Israel, proecipitavit Omnia Moeniaejus discipavit in Filia Juda humiliatumes humiliatam. Lam.lerem. C.D.V.S. tradidis in manu Inimici muro Turnumejus... ogitavit Dominus discipare Murum ejus...Luxitque antimurales murus peritem discipalus est. Defixoe sunt im Terra Portae ejus." - i.e., in rough translation: Lord is created, old enemy ruined Israel, destroyed all his ramparts, dissipated the humiliated daughters of Judas. The lament of Jeremiah C(hapter) F(ive) V(erse) E(leven). He abandoned the ramparts of his tower into the hands of enemy. The Lord created its walls...he lamented over (the bulwark) of Christianity - Belgrade thoroughly destroyed. His gates are stuck to the ground.
