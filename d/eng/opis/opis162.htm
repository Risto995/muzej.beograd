PLAN OF THE SIEGE OF BELGRADE IN 1789 WITH PANORAMA OF BELGRADE AND ZEMUN

This plan is accomplished in coloured ink. It represents an area between Be`anija and Zemun (in its upper part) until Dedinje and eastern Vra~ar (lower part). A cartouche with title and topographic marks for infantry battalions (red-white) and cavalry divisions (green-white fields) is placed to the left. The marks for artillery are not depicted. Plans of four settlements are represented: Be`anija, Zemun , Belgrade (fortress and town) and so-called Cigansko selo (Ziegeuner Dorf, Gypsy village) which is located at the spot occupied by so- called Nova Srpska varo{ in plans from the beginning of XVIII century. Roads and recently constructed embankment with bridges connecting the besiege troops with Zemun are also drawn. Along the river coastline are the roads leading from Belgrade to [abac and Smederevo . Two roads are leading to the south: (the western one) to Banjaluka (obviously incorrect) and another, unreadable one, at the east. A march route of Laudon 's group from Ostru`nica to Belgrade is indicated in red ink, and the camp is placed within renovated contravallation line of Eugenee of Savoy from 171. Earthworks are represented - approaches for the attack of town, as well as those for the attack to the Upper town of Belgrade fortress. To the west of "unreadable" road is the artillery reserve, and Marshall Laudon 's headquarters is located south of it, followed by the residence of prince Francis (subsequent Emperor Francis II, i.e., the first Austrian Emperor Francis I, since 1806.). In addition to the markings of battalions and divisions the plan contains data about commanders, i.e., the place of origin of military units. Legends for positions around the mouth of Sava and Veliko ratno ostrvo (Great war island) marked by numbers 21 - 30 and the letter C are above the text. Below the plan are entries 1-20. Below cartouche in the lower left corner is the panorama of Belgrade and Zemun (18 x 6 cm) titled: A view of the fortress of Belgrade from Serbian side before the siege. The noted view, from eastern Vra~ar, is very rare, and below it stands a legend with (a - f) entries. An outline of Zemun is seen in the background. All legends are in German language.
