BODENEHR'S ALBUM (FIG. 29)
PANORAMA OF IASSI

Only the castle of Iassi is shown in the panorama, along with a bridge by which it is approached, and a part of town is visible within the right margin. In a short description on the left side of panorama, it is noted that it was a capital of Moldavia and the seat of its princes. The town was plundered by Cossacks in ancient times, and it was thereafter thoroughly fortified. However, the Cossacks and Poles took hold of it in 1686 during their campaign against the Tatars. After that, the town was damaged in the large conflagration. Iassi is placed on the river of Prut 25 - 50 miles (cca. 220 km) away from Polish border.
