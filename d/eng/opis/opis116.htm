GEOGRAPHICAL-POLITICAL MAP OF HUNGARY (AND NEIGHBOURING COUNTRIES)

A new map of Hungarian kingdom with provincial borders (in Latin). Apart from Hungary, this map encompasses a part of the neighbouring countries - from Vienna in the west to the Iron Gates in the east, and from central Slovakia in the north to the region of Raška in the south. A sort of topographic explanation is given below the map indicating that forts marked by crescent are occupied by Turks but that the cross above them signifies their belonging to the Christian world. There are two cartouches in the upper left corner, and one cartouche in the lower right corner with text about the authors. The wind rose is in the center of map, and it may be noted that the map orientation is moved for 30 degrees counter- clockwise. Borders are partially marked by colour, and several provincial armours are also included (fairly precise - the armour of Croatia is coloured in black and blue). Above the cartouche , at the lower right is the representation of a battle between Christians and Turks and the text explains that it concerns the unfortunate campaign of the Emperor Sigismund against Mohammed "the seventh Turkish sultan".

Regarding Belgrade, it is explained that its name in Latin was Taurunum (Latin name of Zemun ), and the Latin name of Belgrade - Singidunum was ascribed to Smederevo .
