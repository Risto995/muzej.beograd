Nenadović (Alekse), Prota Matija (1777-1854), writer and politician
Nenadović was educated in Srem. In 1893 became a priest. With his uncle Jakov , started the uprising in Kolubara district in 1804. As a diplomat in restored Serbia, he was the head of missions to Russia (1804) and Austria (1806). He was the president of Praviteljstvujušči Sovjet srbski (Serbian government council). After the downfall of insurrection he had escaped, but returned already in 1814. Participated in the Second Serbian Insurrection and was in opposition to Knez Miloš Obrenović. During the constitutionalist government (ustavobranitelji) he suppressed the " rebellion of katanas ", and in 1848 at Sremski Karlovci he supported the Serbs from Voivodina. He wrote the Memoirs (Memoari)
