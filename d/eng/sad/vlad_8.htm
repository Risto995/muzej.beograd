Habsbourgs (Habsburgs)
German aristocratic family of Swabian origin. During XI-XVIII century they have gathered important possessions, among which was the castle of Habsbourg at Arau (Switzerland). After the rebellion (1291-1315) they were expelled from Switzerland. Rudolph I was the first one from the family stepping to the imperial throne (1273-91). He seized Austria, Styria and Carniola from the Czech king Otocar II and turned them into his private estate. The title of Holy Roman Emperor was in possession of this family since 1438, till 1805 when the Empire was abrogated. They owned the title of Emperor of Austria (since its establishment 1806-1867), and were the emperors of Austria-Hungaria since pact of 1867 until its fall in 1918. During Charles V, apart from German lands, they ruled over the lands of Spanish Crown (sun never sets in Charles' empire), till 1556 when Charles V, fatigued after religious wars, divided the estates between Spanish Habsbourgs and German-Austrian Habsbourgs. The former were given to his son Philip, and the latter to his brother Ferdinand who was already in possession of Hungarian-Croatian crown.
