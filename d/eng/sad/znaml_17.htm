Nenadovi} (Jakova) Jevrem (Brankovina 1793 - Belgrade 1867), voivode of Tamnava
Son-in-law of Milo{ Obrenovi}. Since 1811 he was voivode of Tamnava guarding the border between Sokol and Drina. After the collapse of Serbia in 1813, escapes from the country together with his father, and lives in Russia. He returned to Serbia in 1831 and became the president of tribunal at Valjevo. Later he was the government counselor and lived in Belgrade. His daughter Persida was married to prince Aleksandar Kara|or|evi} and was the mother of king Petar I).
