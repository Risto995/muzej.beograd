Milutinović Sima - Sarajlija (Sarajevo 1791 - Belgrade 1848), writer.
He wrote under the pseudonym Čubro Čojković (Čubro was a name given to him because of his small ears, by the president of Senate during his stay in Montenegro, and he chose the surname Čojković himself, as an attribute of courageous and honest man). His father moved to Zemun and hence Sima finished the Greek school there. Afterwards he was educated at Lycaeum at Sremski Karlovci, but has been expelled from school together with Dimitrije Davidović. Therefore, he had to finish school at Segedin, and in 1809 finally arrived to Serbia. He worked as a clerk in the Council. After Dositej 's death he became a teacher in Velika škola (High school, future University) in 1813, and before downfall of the insurrection he fought on Drina in the unit of Zeka Kapetan. After a short exile he returned to Serbia, and joined the Second Insurrection at Valjevo. He was wounded in the battle of Dublje. Afterwards he worked as a clerk in Narodna kancelarija (National office), but after the execution of Petar Moler and Radić Petrović he fled down the Danube . However, he has been arrested in Vidin, and remained in 15 months long custody. Subsequently, in 1827 he arrived to Montenegro as a teacher of future bishop (vladika) Petar II, and in 1835 he was a member of delegation to Constantinople, with Miloš as its head. Finally, in 1842 he became the minister of education. He published a large number of works: Trojesestarstvo, a poem Srbijanka, Istorija Crne Gore (History of Montenegro), Pjevanja crnogorska i hercegovačka (poems of Montenegro and Hercegovina). Serbian youth at Buda crowned him by laurel wreath as the "national poet". Njegoš dedicated Luča mikrokozma to him, and on the occasion of Sarajlija's death, Njegoš published Sprovod prahu S. Milutinovića (Escort of the ashes of S. Milutinović). Miloš Obrenović appointed him as a sort of official historian of Serbia, and therefore he published Istorija Srbije (History of Serbia).
