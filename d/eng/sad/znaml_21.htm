Obradović Dositej (Čakovo 1739 - Belgrade 1811)
Dositej's christen name was Dimitrije. He remained without parents at an early age and was raised by his uncle. For a while he serves as an apprentice, and then goes to monastery of Hopovo and further to Zagreb, intending to study theology. Refused to embrace Catholicism. Bishop of Plaško (in Gorski kotar) sends him to Dalmatia. Having been an agitated spirit, he arrives at Boka (where he was ordained a priest by bishop Vasilije), and then to Zadar, Trieste, and finally to Halle (Germany) where he began to study. Having ensued university studies at Leipzig, he published in 1783 Život i priključenjija (biography), and Saveti zdravog razuma (common sense advices)...After that he went to France, and subsequently to London staying there for 6 months and learning English. On his return to Leipzig in 1788, Dositej published Basne sa naravoučenijima (fables). He stayed for a while in Serbian colony at Trieste. In 1803 he published Etika ili moralna filozofija (Ethics or philosophy of virtue). He arrived to Serbia and Belgrade in 1807 as a teacher of Karađorđe 's son. Subsequently he became the member of the Council and minister of education. He died at tekija (building at the corner of Zmaja od Noćaja and Višnjićeva) on 3 April 1811, and all his possessions were sold out.
