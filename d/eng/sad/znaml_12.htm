Marković, Knez Sima (Veliki Borak ? - Belgrade 1817), politician and diplomat of the Karađorđe's Serbia
Personal friend of Karađorđe , and hence the Assembly for establishment of Praviteljstvujušči sovjet (Government council) took place at Borak instead of Bogovađa (28 August 1805). After death of J. Katić and V. Čarapić he remained the only important delegate of the district of Belgrade. He was the president of Council (Sovjet) in 1807, and in 1809 he was the head of a mission to Bucharest. In 1811 he became the minister of finances (" popečitelj kase narodne"- minister of people's treasury), and after that, he spent a year in Russia as the head of Insurrection mission. In 1813 Marković participated in battles on Drina, being rather inadequate leader and organizer. After the downfall of insurrection he escaped to Austria, spending some time at Maribor, then arrived to Srem and in 1815 returned to Serbia. Because of conflicts in 1816, Miloš had him arrested and handed over to Marashli Ali Pasha who executed him.
