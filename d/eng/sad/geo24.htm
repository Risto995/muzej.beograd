Temišvar - Temeswar, Timosoara, Timisoara
Town in western Romania, center of culture and economy in the Timisoara Banat. Contrary to its name (Timisoara literally means the town on Tamiš) it is located on Begej and the Begej canal. Since the mid XVI century it was Turkish possession, and for a certain period it was the seat of the Timisoara eyalet. After the treaty of Karlovci ( 1699 ) it remained in Turkish possessions, and the army of Eugene of Savoy captured it in the beginning of Austrian-Turkish war in 1716-18. During XVIII century it was an important cultural and political center of Serbian people, and several Serbian ecclesiastic and public conventions were taking place in it.
