Ferdinand I (ruled 1556-1564)
The Holy Roman Emperor since 1556, when his older brother Emperor Charles V abdicated (having divided the estates between his son Philip who received the lands of Spanish crown and his brother Ferdinad I, who received German lands along with the title of Emperor). After Hungarian defeat at Mohacs (1526), Ferdinand obtained a title of the king of Croatia, Hungaria and Bohemia by means of skilled diplomacy. He led a number of wars with Turkey which supported Jovan Zapolja (Szapolyai Janos, Transylvanian duke) who aspired to Hungarian-Croatian throne.
