Murat II (rules 1421-1451), the sultan
Leads a great war and conquers Thessalonike, Epirus and a large part of Albania between 1427 and 1439. Captures Smederevo. The counterattack pushes him back to Sofia and by treaty of 1444 he returned the possessions to Despot Đurađ. But in the same year, he continued the war and won the battle of Varna, when the commander of Christian troops, Hungarian king Vladislav was killed. Murat died during the campaign against Albanian rebellion led by Skenderbey.
