Hooghe, Romeyn de, (Amsterdam 1645 - Harlem 1708)
Draftsman, painter, and engraver, distinguished as the author of battle scenes. Since 1662 he was the member of painters' guild at Hague. Subsequently, he shared his working time between Amsterdam and Hague, and finally settles down at Harlem (1687) where he had opened a school for painters. However, he continued to publish engravings and make illustrations for books.
