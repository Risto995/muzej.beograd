Anastasijević Miša (Poreč na Dunavu 1803 - Bucharest 1885)
Anastasijević was remained without parents at an early age, raised by his step mother. When he was less than 10 years old he worked as a teacher, when at 12 he started to trade wood, having engaged in many other occupations in subsequent years. After he was married, Anastasijević raised the funds and started a stock trade, afterwards he bought a ship and started the salt trade. He received the title of " Danube captain" from Knez Miloš - hence "kapetan Miša". He purchased the estates in Wallachia (in Serbia it was not possible because Miloš suppressed the creation of latifundia in order to protect the small farm lots), supported diligent and clever people, and education in general. His greatest favour to his countrymen was donation of "Kapetan Mišino zdanje", a building at Velika pijaca (today's Studentski trg) in which the Rectorate of Belgrade University is placed nowadays.
