Jovanovi} (Jovana) Anastas (Vrace in Bulgaria 1817- Belgrade 1899)
The first Serbian photographer, lithographer, painter, and since 1861-68, the majordomo of prince's Mihailo Obrenovi} Court. He arrived to Belgrade to his uncle's who was the tailor of Knez Milo{ . After the State Printing house was opened at Belgrade (1832), he was interested in the art of printing and became the apprentice (typefounder?). After the printing house was transferred to Kragujevac (1834) he also moved there. Later he studied in Vienna, first with Theodor Tirka (whose son Demetar took care of the finances of Knez Milo{ ), and in 1838 he attended the drawing course of professor Gsehofer at the Academy. He studied the art of lithography by lithographer Stadler. Around 1840 he bought a Voigtlander camera with serial production number 3. In Vienna 1844, he was the secretary of exiled Knez Milo{ . In 1846 started his work on "Spomenici serbski" /Serbian monuments/. After several unsuccessful attempt, finally obtained a permission to return to Serbia in 1850. After the return of Obrenovi} dynasty, he became the majordomo of prince's Mihailo Court in 1861, and stayed in that assignment until Mihailo's death in 1868. Afterwards he retreated from public affairs. He became the member of distinguished Viennese society of photographers in 1877.
