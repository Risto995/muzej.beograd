Ortelius Abraham (1527-1598) cartographer
Famous cartographer from Antwerp. He made a map of world on 8 sheets (1564), a map of Egypt, etc. His most significant work "Theatrum orbis terrarum" was published in May 1570. In the same year it had four edition in Latin language. Until 1612 it had 16 editions in Latin, 2 editions in Dutch, 5 editions in German, 6 editions in Spanish and 2 editions in Italian language. He started rather prosperously as the salesman of maps and engravings, as well as the painter at the age of 20, supported by his sister. Already in 1575 he became the cartographer of Philip II (son of the Spanish king Charles V). Traveling around Europe he arrived to London and assisted to William Camden to publish "Britannia" (1856). He was finely educated artisan and has published several various maps (compilations), but his work was crowned by "Theatrum Orbis Terrarum", printed in well known printing firm of Cristopher Plantin. Original edition of that work consisted of 70 maps, i.e., a map of world, 4 maps of continents, 3 (regional) maps of Africa, 6 maps of Asia and large number of maps of Europe. The first edition contained works of 87 collaborators, and the edition from 1603 included even 183 collaborators. It represented the first, modern, richly illustrated atlas which has reached a very high artistic standard. After 1579, Ortelius added to the atlas a collection of old maps "Paregon Theatri". His work was published after his death as well as "Ortelius redivivus et continuatus".
