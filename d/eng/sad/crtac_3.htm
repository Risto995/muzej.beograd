Gumpp, Johann Baptista (1651-1728) engineer
Member of a large family of architects, engineers, copper engravers and painters who were active between XVI and XVIII century. His first work was a map of Tyrol with portraits of landlords, then followed allegorical figures and armours. For a long time he worked in the civil and military engineer service of Maximilian Emmanuel of Bavaria, the elected prince, and for some time also in the service of emperor Leopold as the chief engineer and treasurer at Constance.
