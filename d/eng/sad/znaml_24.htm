Simeonović Čolak Anta (Prizren, around 1777 - Kragujevac 1853)
Arrived to Serbia long before insurrection as a fur dresser and merchant. In the eve of insurrection goes to Prizren and buys a load of guns and hands them over to Karađorđe . He was wounded in arm during a conflict at Ostružnica (1806), having remained disabled and since then gets a nick-name Čolak (in Turkish language meaning disabled, crippled, one-armed). After the conquest of Sjenica (1809) he connected the insurrectors with Vasojevići, and in 1811 became the voivode of Kruševac. However, after the insurrection downfall he had to escape to Austria, and 1814 arrived to Russia. Čolak Anta returned to Serbia in 1830 and became a member of district tribunal. In 1835 he was retired and lived in Kruševac and Kragujevac till the end of his life.
