Suleiman (II) the Magnificent (tur. 'Kanuni' - Lawgiver, ruled 1520-1566), the sultan
The greatest Turkish ruler, conquered Rhodes, Belgrade ( 1521 ), and also defeated the Hungarians at Mohacs, when the last Hungarian king was killed. He conquered a large part of northern Africa, while his fleet commanded by the admiral - pirate Khair ed-Din Barbarossa was looting over Mediterranean. He was a great rival of German emperor and Spanish king Charles V. Vienna was besieged for the first time under his command (1529). His epoch is marked by establishment of many laws and regulations, but also by the government of great vizier Mehmed Sokolović.
